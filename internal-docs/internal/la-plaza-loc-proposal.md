## Executive Summary

Elevate for Humanity proposes four LOC cohort-based certification pathways designed for working adults, with bilingual support, employer-integrated training, and structured reporting for case management.

**Programs offered for LOC cohorts:**

- **Forklift Operator Certification** (OSHA-compliant): 40 hours, 1–2 weeks
- **HVAC Technician Pathway** + EPA 608 Universal Prep: 12 weeks, 144 hours
- **Electrical Technician Pathway** + OSHA 10 Construction: 12 weeks, 144 hours
- **Plumbing Technician Pathway**: 12 weeks, 144 hours

**What makes Elevate different:**

1. **Completion-focused structure.** Evening classes + employer site days + LMS coursework, with tutoring and rapid intervention triggers for attendance and performance.

2. **Reporting built for workforce case management.** Bi-weekly progress reports with attendance %, module completion %, assessment scores, and instructor notes; optional Salesforce-ready exports.

3. **Career pathway design (not "just a certificate").** Each cohort includes employer site visits, job shadowing, apprenticeship pathway guidance, and placement support so participants don't walk away with only OSHA 10.

**Cohort launch timeline:** Custom LOC cohort can launch in **2–3 weeks** from agreement, contingent on participant roster confirmation and schedule selection.

## What Makes This Different

### Apprenticeship Pipeline — Not Just Certification

Every Elevate trades program ends with apprenticeship application guidance, employer referral support, and documented training hours. Participants do not walk away with a card and a handshake. They leave with:

- Verified instructional hours documented for apprenticeship credit
- Apprenticeship application guidance and referral support to Indianapolis-area union and non-union sponsors
- Employer site experience through structured job shadowing and site visits during the program
- Resume, interview prep, and employer introductions completed before program end
- 30/90/180-day post-placement follow-up with outcomes reported to LOC

This is the piece most training vendors skip. It is the piece that makes your grant outcomes measurable.

## Delivery Model: Classroom + LMS + Employer Site Days

Elevate uses a **Training + Third-Party Testing + Employer Integration** model:

| Component               | Elevate Delivers                                         | Third-Party Partner                                    |
| ----------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Theory instruction**  | Classroom (Mon/Wed evenings)                             | —                                                      |
| **LMS coursework**      | Self-paced video lessons, quizzes, progress tracking     | —                                                      |
| **Employer site days**  | Coordinated job shadowing, site visits, externship hours | Employer partners host                                 |
| **Certification exams** | Exam preparation and scheduling                          | EPA-approved testing center (EPA 608); OSHA (Forklift) |
| **Career placement**    | Resume, interview prep, employer matching                | Employer partners hire                                 |

This model gives participants real employer exposure during training — not after — which accelerates placement and builds documented work experience hours.

### HVAC Technician Pathway + EPA 608 Prep

|                      |                                                                                                                                                           |
| -------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Length**           | 12 weeks                                                                                                                                                  |
| **Hours**            | 144 instructional hours                                                                                                                                   |
| **Schedule**         | Mon & Wed, 5:30–8:30 PM (classroom, 6 hrs/week) + every other Saturday employer site day (6 hrs, avg 3 hrs/week) + LMS coursework (3 hrs/week self-paced) |
| **Credential**       | EPA Section 608 Universal Certification + HVAC Technician Certificate of Completion                                                                       |
| **EPA 608 exam**     | Administered by EPA-approved testing center (Mainstream Engineering / ESCO voucher)                                                                       |
| **Top Jobs Indiana** | **4-star** (SOC 49-9021) — high demand, high wage                                                                                                         |
| **Next Level Jobs**  | Eligible                                                                                                                                                  |
| **Cost**             | **$2,700 per participant**                                                                                                                                |

**Why HVAC:** 4-star Indiana demand rating. Median salary $50K+. Year-round work. Apprenticeship application support and referral to Indianapolis-area mechanical contractors. EPA 608 is a knowledge-based certification — no lab equipment required for the exam itself. Field exposure occurs through employer site days and documented observation hours.

**Curriculum (10 modules):**

1. Introduction to HVAC — industry overview, Indiana licensing, career pathways
2. Heating Fundamentals — gas furnaces, electric heat, combustion analysis theory
3. Cooling and Refrigeration — refrigeration cycle, R-410A, superheat/subcooling
4. Electrical for HVAC — wiring diagrams, motors, capacitors, control circuits
5. Air Distribution and Ductwork — airflow principles, duct sizing, air balancing
6. Heat Pumps and Dual-Fuel Systems — air-source, geothermal, mini-splits
7. HVAC Controls and Thermostats — smart thermostats, zone systems, building automation
8. HVAC Safety and EPA 608 Certification Prep — OSHA, LOTO, refrigerant handling, EPA exam prep
9. HVAC Service and Troubleshooting — systematic diagnostics, preventive maintenance
10. Indiana Licensing and Career Launch — apprenticeship applications, employer introductions, job placement

**Employer site days (6 sessions):** Participants visit HVAC contractor job sites to observe residential and commercial installations, service calls, and equipment. Documented hours count toward apprenticeship credit applications.

**EPA 608 exam:** Scheduled at EPA-approved testing center during weeks 11–12. Exam voucher included in program cost.

## Certification Testing Model

Elevate prepares participants for certification exams. Exams are administered by approved third-party testing centers:

| Program        | Certification                            | Testing Partner                                        | Exam Format                                         |
| -------------- | ---------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------- |
| **HVAC**       | EPA Section 608 Universal                | Mainstream Engineering (EPATest.com) or ESCO Institute | Proctored knowledge exam (online or testing center) |
| **Forklift**   | OSHA Forklift Operator (29 CFR 1910.178) | Employer partner site                                  | Written exam + practical driving evaluation         |
| **Electrical** | OSHA 10-Hour Construction Safety         | CareerSafe (online)                                    | Online proctored exam                               |
| **Plumbing**   | Program completion certificate           | Elevate                                                | Portfolio-based assessment                          |

All exam fees are included in program pricing. Participants are scheduled for exams during the final 1–2 weeks of the program.
