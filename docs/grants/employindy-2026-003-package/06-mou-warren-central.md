## I. PURPOSE

This MOU establishes a partnership between Elevate for Humanity and Warren Central High School to support the delivery of WIOA-funded In-School Youth career coaching, pre-apprenticeship, and workforce readiness services to eligible students during the 2026–2027 school year, contingent upon award of EmployIndy RFP 2026-003.

The goal of this partnership is to connect Warren Central students ages 16–19 with documented barriers to high-demand career pathways, industry-recognized credentials, and employment opportunities in Marion County.

---

## II. TERM

This MOU is effective upon execution by both parties and covers the period of **July 1, 2026 through June 30, 2027**, with the option to renew for up to two successive years through June 30, 2029, contingent upon continued funding and mutual agreement.

---

## III. BACKGROUND

Elevate for Humanity is a Marion County nonprofit workforce development organization and existing EmployIndy and WorkOne partner. Elevate holds active DOL Registered Apprenticeship Program (RAP) status (Provider ID 206251) for the Building Services Technician occupation and operates a proprietary AI-enabled Learning Management System delivering HVAC curriculum and career readiness content to participants across Marion County.

Warren Central High School serves a diverse student population in MSD Warren Township, Indianapolis, including students with documented barriers to employment and postsecondary success. This partnership leverages Elevate's workforce infrastructure and Warren Central's school-based support systems to create a seamless pathway from high school enrollment to industry credential and employment.

---

## IV. ELEVATE FOR HUMANITY COMMITMENTS

Elevate for Humanity agrees to:

1. **Assign a dedicated Career Coach** (Ameco Martin) to Warren Central High School to deliver in-person career coaching, Individual Service Strategy (ISS) development, and WIOA enrollment services during school hours on a scheduled basis agreed upon with school administration.

2. **Deliver all 14 WIOA Youth Service Elements** to enrolled participants, including career exploration, occupational skills training, work-based learning, financial literacy, leadership development, and postsecondary transition support.

3. **Provide HVAC pre-apprenticeship pathway** through Elevate's DOL-registered Building Services Technician RAP (Provider ID 206251), including EPA 608 Universal Certification exam preparation and testing fee coverage at no cost to participants.

4. **Cover all participant costs** including transportation stipends, work attire, tools, and industry certification exam fees through WIOA supportive services funding. No cost is passed to participants or to Warren Central High School.

5. **Enroll all eligible participants in WIOA** and maintain accurate records in Indiana Career Connect in compliance with EmployIndy and Indiana DWD policy.

6. **Co-enroll all participants in Project Indy** per EmployIndy requirements.

7. **Coordinate with Warren Central staff** including Intervention Specialist Nicole Simpson to identify WIOA-eligible students with documented barriers and ensure wraparound support is in place.

8. **Conduct monthly coordination meetings** with Warren Central's designated point of contact to review participant progress, address barriers, and align services with school priorities.

9. **Provide 12-month follow-up services** for all program completers, tracking employment, education, and training outcomes in Indiana Career Connect.

10. **Maintain full WIOA compliance** including equal opportunity requirements under 29 CFR Part 38, data privacy standards, and all applicable federal, state, and local regulations.

11. **Carry appropriate organizational insurance** and maintain financial controls meeting federal grant management standards.

12. **Provide Warren Central with quarterly progress reports** on participant outcomes, credential attainment, and employment placement.

---

## V. WARREN CENTRAL HIGH SCHOOL COMMITMENTS

Warren Central High School agrees to:

1. **Designate school-based points of contact** — Principal Masimba Taylor and Intervention Specialist Nicole Simpson — to coordinate with Elevate's Career Coach on student referrals, scheduling, and program logistics.

2. **Provide access to school facilities** for Career Coach Ameco Martin to meet with students during school hours in a private, appropriate setting.

3. **Refer eligible students** — ages 16–19 with documented barriers including low income, basic skills deficiency, foster care status, or justice involvement — to Elevate's Career Coach for WIOA eligibility screening.

4. **Support participant recruitment** by allowing Elevate to conduct classroom presentations, career exploration sessions, and outreach activities on campus.

5. **Collaborate with Elevate staff** on Individual Service Strategy development and student progress monitoring.

6. **Share relevant student information** in compliance with FERPA and applicable privacy laws to support WIOA eligibility documentation and service delivery.

7. **Participate in monthly coordination meetings** with Elevate's Career Coach and program director.

---

## VI. SHARED COMMITMENTS

Both parties agree to:

1. Maintain confidentiality of all participant personally identifiable information (PII) in compliance with FERPA, WIOA privacy requirements, and applicable state and federal law. No PII will be transmitted via email.

2. Communicate openly and promptly regarding participant needs, program changes, and any concerns affecting service delivery.

3. Operate in a manner consistent with equity, trauma-informed practice, and culturally responsive service delivery.

4. Comply with all applicable equal opportunity and non-discrimination requirements.

---

## VII. FUNDING & FINANCIAL TERMS

This MOU is contingent upon Elevate for Humanity receiving an award under EmployIndy RFP 2026-003. No financial obligation is created by this MOU prior to contract execution with EmployIndy. **Warren Central High School incurs no financial obligation under this MOU.** All program costs are borne by Elevate for Humanity through WIOA funding.

---

## VIII. DATA SHARING & PRIVACY

Participant data shared between parties will be limited to what is necessary for program administration and WIOA compliance. All data sharing will comply with FERPA, WIOA regulations, and Indiana state privacy law. No participant PII will be transmitted via email. Elevate's LMS stores participant data on SOC 2-compliant infrastructure with role-based access controls.

---

## IX. MODIFICATION AND TERMINATION

Either party may terminate this MOU with 30 days written notice. Modifications to this MOU must be agreed upon in writing by both parties.
