## Line Item Budget & Narrative

### Personnel — $120,000

**Career Coach — Ameco Martin (1.0 FTE) — $52,000**
Ameco Martin serves as the dedicated Career Coach embedded at Warren Central High School. Responsibilities include: in-person career coaching, WIOA eligibility screening, ISS development and updates in Indiana Career Connect, employer coordination, work-based learning placement, monthly participant check-ins, and EmployIndy coordination meetings. Full-time position, 12 months.

**Elizabeth Greene — CEO / Program Director / Case Manager (0.75 FTE) — $68,000**
Elizabeth Greene serves as Case Manager, responsible for WIOA enrollment documentation, Indiana Career Connect data entry and maintenance, participant follow-up at 30/90/180/365 days post-exit, barrier coordination, supportive services disbursement, and quarterly performance reporting. Full-time position, 12 months.

---

### LMS Platform & Technology — $24,000

Covers Elevate's proprietary LMS platform hosting, maintenance, AI-assisted content delivery infrastructure, participant dashboard reporting, Indiana Career Connect data export tools, and technology support. The LMS is the primary RTI delivery vehicle for all 80 participants and the source of real-time measurable skills gain data. Cost is $2,000/month × 12 months.

---

---

### Work-Based Learning Coordination — $50,000

Covers employer engagement activities, job shadowing coordination, OJT placement logistics, employer panel events at Warren Central, employer onboarding documentation, WBL tracking and reporting, and mileage reimbursement for Career Coach travel between Warren Central and employer sites.

---

### Training & Curriculum — $24,000

Covers HVAC curriculum content licensing and updates, employability bootcamp materials, financial literacy module development, labor market information session materials, and EPA 608 exam preparation resources. Content is delivered via Elevate's LMS — no facility rental costs.

---

### Administrative Costs — $38,000 (9.5%)

Covers financial management, grant compliance, audit preparation, organizational insurance, office supplies, and indirect costs. Administrative costs are held to 5.5% of total budget, well within federal reasonableness standards for WIOA-funded contracts.
