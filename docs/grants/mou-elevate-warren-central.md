# MEMORANDUM OF UNDERSTANDING

## Elevate for Humanity & Warren Central High School

## WIOA In-School Youth Career Coaching Partnership

---

**This Memorandum of Understanding ("MOU") is entered into between:**

**Elevate for Humanity**
A nonprofit 501(c)(3) workforce development organization
Indianapolis, Indiana
www.elevateforhumanity.org
Contact: Elizabeth Greene, Founder & Executive Director

**AND**

**Warren Central High School**
MSD Warren Township
Indianapolis, Indiana
Contact: Masimba Taylor, Principal

---

## I. PURPOSE

This MOU establishes a partnership between Elevate for Humanity and Warren Central High School to support the delivery of WIOA-funded In-School Youth career coaching, pre-apprenticeship, and workforce readiness services to eligible students during the 2026–2027 school year, contingent upon award of EmployIndy RFP 2026-003.

The goal of this partnership is to connect Warren Central students ages 16–19 with documented barriers to high-demand career pathways, industry-recognized credentials, and employment opportunities in Marion County.

---

## II. TERM

This MOU is effective upon execution by both parties and covers the period of **July 1, 2026 through June 30, 2027**, with the option to renew for up to two successive years through June 30, 2029, contingent upon continued funding and mutual agreement.

---

## III. ELEVATE FOR HUMANITY COMMITMENTS

Elevate for Humanity agrees to:

1. **Assign a dedicated Career Coach** (Ameco Martin) to Warren Central High School to deliver in-person career coaching, Individual Service Strategy (ISS) development, and WIOA enrollment services during school hours on a scheduled basis.

2. **Deliver all 14 WIOA Youth Service Elements** to enrolled participants, including career exploration, occupational skills training, work-based learning, financial literacy, leadership development, and postsecondary transition support.

3. **Provide HVAC pre-apprenticeship pathway** through Elevate's DOL-registered Building Services Technician RAP (Provider ID 206251), including EPA 608 Universal Certification exam preparation and testing fee coverage.

4. **Cover all participant costs** including transportation stipends, work attire, tools, and industry certification exam fees through WIOA supportive services funding.

5. **Enroll all eligible participants in WIOA** and maintain accurate records in Indiana Career Connect in compliance with EmployIndy and Indiana DWD policy.

6. **Coordinate with Warren Central staff** including intervention specialists and school counselors to identify WIOA-eligible students with documented barriers and ensure wraparound support.

7. **Conduct monthly check-ins** with Warren Central's designated point of contact to review participant progress, address barriers, and align services with school priorities.

8. **Provide 12-month follow-up services** for all program completers, tracking employment, education, and training outcomes in Indiana Career Connect.

9. **Maintain full WIOA compliance** including equal opportunity requirements, data privacy standards, and all applicable federal, state, and local regulations.

10. **Carry appropriate insurance** and maintain organizational financial controls meeting federal grant management standards.

---

## IV. WARREN CENTRAL HIGH SCHOOL COMMITMENTS

Warren Central High School agrees to:

1. **Designate a school-based point of contact** to coordinate with Elevate's Career Coach on student referrals, scheduling, and program logistics.

2. **Provide access to school facilities** for Career Coach to meet with students during school hours in a private, appropriate setting.

3. **Refer eligible students** — ages 16–19 with documented barriers including low income, basic skills deficiency, foster care status, or justice involvement — to Elevate's Career Coach for WIOA eligibility screening.

4. **Support participant recruitment** by allowing Elevate to conduct classroom presentations, career exploration sessions, and outreach activities on campus.

5. **Collaborate with Elevate staff** on Individual Service Strategy development and student progress monitoring.

6. **Share relevant student information** in compliance with FERPA and applicable privacy laws to support WIOA eligibility documentation and service delivery.

---

## V. SHARED COMMITMENTS

Both parties agree to:

1. Maintain confidentiality of all participant personally identifiable information (PII) in compliance with FERPA, WIOA privacy requirements, and applicable state and federal law.

2. Communicate openly and promptly regarding participant needs, program changes, and any concerns affecting service delivery.

3. Participate in monthly coordination meetings and quarterly program reviews.

4. Operate in a manner consistent with equity, trauma-informed practice, and culturally responsive service delivery.

---

## VI. FUNDING

This MOU is contingent upon Elevate for Humanity receiving an award under EmployIndy RFP 2026-003. No financial obligation is created by this MOU prior to contract execution with EmployIndy. Warren Central High School incurs no financial obligation under this MOU.

---

## VII. MODIFICATION AND TERMINATION

Either party may terminate this MOU with 30 days written notice. Modifications to this MOU must be agreed upon in writing by both parties.
