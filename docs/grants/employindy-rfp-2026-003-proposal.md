## SECTION 2: ORGANIZATIONAL OVERVIEW & CAPACITY

_(15 points)_

Elevate for Humanity is a Marion County nonprofit workforce development organization founded to expand access to high-demand career pathways for underserved adults and youth. Our mission is to eliminate barriers between Indianapolis residents and living-wage employment through technology-enabled training, registered apprenticeship, and wraparound case management.

**Registered Apprenticeship Provider:** Elevate holds active DOL Registered Apprenticeship Program (RAP) status as a Training Agent under Provider ID 206251 for the Building Services Technician occupation, which includes HVAC systems, refrigeration, and EPA 608 certification. This is a federally registered, DOL-compliant credential pathway — not a proposed program, but an operational one.

**Proprietary LMS Platform:** Elevate operates a proprietary Learning Management System (LMS) built on modern web infrastructure, delivering asynchronous online instruction accessible on any device — phone, tablet, or computer. The LMS tracks lesson completion, checkpoint scores, measurable skills gain, and time-on-task at the individual participant level, generating real-time performance data exportable for WIOA reporting.

**Case Management Infrastructure:** Elevate employs a dedicated case manager responsible for participant enrollment, documentation, progress tracking, and follow-up. This infrastructure is operational and will be extended to serve WIOA In-School Youth participants under this contract.

**Career Coach:** Ameco Martin serves as Elevate's designated Career Coach for this program. Ameco brings direct experience in career development and youth workforce engagement and will be the primary point of contact for participants at Robert Lee Frost School 109.

**Administrative Readiness:** Elevate maintains financial controls, program documentation systems, and reporting infrastructure capable of managing WIOA-funded contracts. We are prepared to enter all participants in Indiana Career Connect, comply with EmployIndy WIOA policy, and submit required performance data on schedule.

**School Partnership:** Elevate has established a partnership with Robert Lee Frost School 109 (IPS) in Indianapolis. School Psychologist Malkah Bird, EdS, NCSP serves as our school-based point of contact, supporting participant identification, barrier assessment, and referral coordination.

## SECTION 3: PROGRAM DESIGN & INTEGRATED SERVICE DELIVERY

_(15 points)_

Elevate's service model moves participants through a clear, technology-enabled journey from first contact to credential attainment and employment. Every step is documented in Indiana Career Connect and tracked in Elevate's LMS.

**Customer Flow:**

**Entry → Screening → Enrollment → Training → Credential → Employment → Follow-Up**

**Step 1 — Entry & Outreach**
Career Coach Ameco Martin is embedded at Frost School 109, conducting in-person outreach during school hours. Elevate also reaches students through school counselor referrals (coordinated with Malkah Bird, EdS, NCSP), classroom presentations, and digital outreach via Elevate's website and social platforms. Recruitment targets WIOA-eligible youth ages 16–19 with documented barriers including low income, basic skills deficiency, foster care status, or justice involvement.

**Step 2 — WIOA Eligibility Screening & Enrollment**
Career Coach conducts WIOA eligibility determination using EmployIndy's approved eligibility policy. All eligible participants are enrolled in WIOA and entered in Indiana Career Connect within required timeframes. All participants are co-enrolled in Project Indy.

**Step 3 — Objective Assessment & Individual Service Strategy (ISS)**
Each participant receives a comprehensive objective assessment covering academic level, career interests, labor market awareness, and barrier inventory. The Career Coach develops an Individual Service Strategy (ISS) in Indiana Career Connect linking each participant's goals to specific WIOA service elements, career pathways, and performance milestones. The ISS is treated as a living document, reviewed and updated at 30, 60, and 90-day intervals.

**Step 4 — Service Delivery**
Participants access all 14 WIOA Youth Service Elements through Elevate's integrated model (detailed in the WIOA 14 Service Elements Plan). Core delivery includes:

- In-person career coaching at Frost School 109 with Career Coach Ameco Martin
- Asynchronous online instruction via Elevate's LMS (accessible on any device)
- HVAC pre-apprenticeship pathway with EPA 608 exam preparation
- Employability skills bootcamp (workplace behavior, financial literacy, professionalism)
- Employer engagement events with 2Exclusive Platinum Cleaning and HVAC industry partners

**Step 5 — Credential Attainment**
Participants on the HVAC pathway sit for the EPA 608 Universal Certification exam. Testing fees are covered under supportive services. Elevate's LMS checkpoint system assesses exam readiness before registration, maximizing pass rates. EPA 608 is a federally recognized, industry-required credential that counts directly toward the WIOA Credential Attainment Rate.

**Step 6 — Employment Placement**
Career Coach coordinates job placement with 2Exclusive Platinum Cleaning and HVAC employer partners in Marion County. Participants not entering HVAC employment are connected to postsecondary education or other training pathways aligned with their ISS goals.

**Step 7 — Follow-Up**
Case manager conducts follow-up contact at 30, 90, 180, and 365 days post-exit. Follow-up data is entered in Indiana Career Connect to support WIOA 2Q and 4Q employment retention measures.

**Technology & Innovation:**
Elevate's LMS uses AI-assisted content delivery, automated progress tracking, and real-time dashboard reporting. Career Coaches receive automated alerts when participants fall behind on milestones, enabling proactive intervention before dropout. This directly addresses EmployIndy's stated goal of using AI-enabled decision-support tools to improve outcomes and accelerate movement through WIOA service sequencing.

## SECTION 4: EMPLOYER ENGAGEMENT, TRAINING & CAREER PATHWAYS

_(15 points)_

**Primary Employer Partner: 2Exclusive Platinum Cleaning**
2Exclusive Platinum Cleaning is Elevate's OJT employer partner for the Building Services Technician RAP. They provide paid work-based learning placements, on-site mentorship, and a direct hiring pipeline for program completers. This is an existing, operational partnership — not a proposed one.

**Career Pathway: HVAC Building Services Technician**
HVAC is consistently identified on Indiana's Demand Occupation List. The U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics projects 6% national growth for HVAC mechanics through 2032. In Marion County, aging commercial infrastructure and rapid construction growth drive sustained employer demand. EPA 608 certification is a federal legal requirement for any technician handling refrigerants — creating a hard credential barrier that limits supply and elevates wages above the WIOA median earnings target of $4,300 in Q2.

**Pathway Structure:**

- High school enrollment → WIOA eligibility → ISS development
- HVAC pre-apprenticeship: online RTI via Elevate LMS + OJT at 2Exclusive
- EPA 608 Universal Certification exam (fees covered)
- DOL Certificate of Completion upon RAP completion
- Employment in HVAC at 2Exclusive or placement with Marion County HVAC employers
- Stackable credentials toward BOMA, IFMA, and Indiana occupational certifications

**Employer Engagement Activities:**

- Employer panels and industry tours for participants at Frost School 109
- Job shadowing placements at 2Exclusive Platinum Cleaning
- Employer-led professionalism and workplace readiness sessions
- Hiring commitments documented in MOU with 2Exclusive

**Labor Market Information Delivery:**
Career Coach delivers sector-based career exploration sessions at Frost School 109 covering HVAC industry demand, wage progression, and career ladder from pre-apprentice to journeyworker to facilities manager. All LMI content is aligned with Indiana's high-demand occupation data.

## SECTION 5: PARTICIPANT SUPPORT, RETENTION & BARRIER REMOVAL

_(5 points)_

Elevate's retention model is built into program design at every stage:

**Asynchronous Online Delivery:** All RTI is delivered via Elevate's LMS on any device, eliminating transportation barriers to instruction. Participants complete coursework on their own schedule around school and work hours.

**Dedicated Case Manager:** Monthly check-ins for every participant. At-risk flags triggered automatically by LMS inactivity alerts. Structured re-engagement plan before any disenrollment action.

**Supportive Services (allowable under WIOA):**

- Transportation stipends for work-based learning participation
- Work attire and tools for OJT placements
- EPA 608 exam fees covered
- Multilingual support and accessibility accommodations as needed
- Participant incentives for milestone completion

**School-Based Support Coordination:** Career Coach Ameco Martin works directly with school psychologist Malkah Bird, EdS, NCSP to identify students with barriers, coordinate trauma-informed referrals, and ensure participants receive school-based wraparound support alongside Elevate's workforce services.

**Mentor Assignment:** Each participant on the HVAC pathway is assigned a qualified mentor at 2Exclusive Platinum Cleaning providing weekly supervision, feedback, and professional development guidance.

## SECTION 6: EQUITY, TRAUMA-INFORMED & CULTURALLY RESPONSIVE PRACTICE

_(10 points)_

**Organizational Identity:** Elevate for Humanity is a woman-owned, Black-led nonprofit organization. Our leadership reflects the communities we serve. Equity is not a program add-on — it is the founding premise of the organization.

**Trauma-Informed Practice:** Elevate's Career Coach and case manager are trained in trauma-informed engagement principles. We recognize that youth with documented barriers — low income, justice involvement, foster care, housing instability — require relationship-first service delivery before workforce readiness activities can be effective. Our ISS process begins with barrier identification and trust-building, not immediate program enrollment.

**School Psychologist Partnership:** Our partnership with Malkah Bird, EdS, NCSP at Frost School 109 ensures that participants with mental health, behavioral, or trauma-related barriers receive coordinated support from a licensed school psychologist alongside Elevate's workforce services. This is a genuine clinical-workforce integration, not a referral list.

**Culturally Responsive Service Delivery:**

- All program materials available in plain language
- Career Coach Ameco Martin reflects the cultural background of the target population
- Recruitment and outreach conducted through trusted community channels
- Program design centers participant voice — ISS goals are participant-driven, not provider-driven

**Equity in Credential Access:** EPA 608 exam fees are covered for all participants. No participant is denied credential attainment due to inability to pay testing fees.

**Target Population:** Elevate prioritizes recruitment of youth from high-need Marion County ZIP codes, including students at Frost School 109 (IPS), with emphasis on youth experiencing low income, basic skills deficiency, foster care involvement, or justice system contact.

## SECTION 7: PERFORMANCE MEASUREMENT, DATA & CONTINUOUS IMPROVEMENT

_(10 points)_

**Data Systems:**

- **Indiana Career Connect:** All participants enrolled and tracked. ISS documented, updated, and linked to performance measures. Career Coach enters all required data in compliance with EmployIndy and DWD policy.
- **Elevate LMS:** Real-time tracking of lesson completion, checkpoint scores, measurable skills gain, and time-on-task. Automated alerts for at-risk participants. Data exportable in CSV for WIOA reporting.
- **Program Dashboard:** Monthly aggregate metrics reviewed by program director — retention rates, credential attainment, employment placement, LMS completion percentages.

**Continuous Improvement Cycle:**

- Monthly: Career Coach reviews individual participant progress in ICC and LMS. At-risk participants receive intervention within 48 hours of flag.
- Quarterly: Program director reviews aggregate performance against WIOA targets. Findings documented in quarterly report. Corrective actions triggered for any metric below threshold.
- Annually: Full program review incorporating all four quarters. Curriculum updates, recruitment adjustments, and staffing changes informed by data.

**EmployIndy Coordination:**

- Monthly meetings with EmployIndy point of contact attended by Career Coach and program director
- Quarterly performance reports submitted on schedule
- All ICC data entries completed within required timeframes

**AI-Enabled Decision Support:**
Elevate's LMS generates automated participant risk scores based on login frequency, assignment completion rates, and checkpoint performance. Career Coach receives weekly dashboard summaries identifying participants requiring outreach. This reduces dropout by enabling intervention before disengagement becomes withdrawal.
