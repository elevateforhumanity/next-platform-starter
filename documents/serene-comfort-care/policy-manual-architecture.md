# SECTION 1 — INTRODUCTION

Serene Comfort Home Care LLC is committed to providing high-quality, compassionate, and personalized home care services. Our mission is to enhance the quality of life for our clients while ensuring full compliance with all Indiana State Department of Health (ISDH) regulations for Home Health Agencies and Personal Services Agencies (PSA), Indiana Health Coverage Programs (IHCP) Medicaid standards, and applicable federal requirements including HIPAA and OSHA.

This manual governs all operations of Serene Comfort Home Care LLC. It applies to all employees, contractors, and volunteers. Every staff member is required to read, understand, and follow these policies. Questions about any policy are directed to the Administrator.

**Policy Format:** Each policy follows this structure:
- **Policy Statement** — The rule
- **Procedures** — How to carry it out
- **Responsible Staff** — Who owns it
- **Required Documentation** — What records must exist

This manual is reviewed annually and updated upon any regulatory change or operational need. Staff are notified in writing of any material policy update and sign an acknowledgment of receipt.

# SECTION 2 — CLIENT CARE PLAN POLICY

**Policy Statement:**
Each client receives an individualized care plan that documents their assessed needs, personal goals, and the specific services to be provided. The care plan is the primary guide for all caregiving activities.

**Procedures:**
1. An initial client assessment is completed within 48 hours of service initiation
2. A written care plan is developed in consultation with the client and, where applicable, the client's family or authorized representative
3. The care plan includes: personal care tasks, nutrition/hydration assistance, mobility assistance, medication reminders (non-skilled), emotional support, and any other authorized services
4. The care plan specifies the level of assistance required for each task (verbal cue, standby assist, or full assist)
5. The care plan is signed by the client or authorized representative before services begin
6. The care plan is reviewed and updated every 90 days or whenever the client's condition or needs change
7. All assigned caregivers receive a copy of the care plan and confirm receipt in writing
8. Caregivers document any deviation from the care plan in the visit record and notify the supervisor the same day

**Responsible Staff:** Administrator / Supervisor (development), all direct care staff (follow and document)

**Required Documentation:** Signed initial assessment, signed care plan, caregiver receipt acknowledgment, amendment records with dates and reason for change

# SECTION 3 — CUSTOMER COMPLAINT POLICY

**Policy Statement:**
Every client and family member has the right to submit a complaint about services at any time without fear of retaliation. Serene Comfort Home Care LLC takes all complaints seriously and resolves them promptly and fairly.

**Procedures:**
1. Complaints may be submitted in person, by phone, in writing, or anonymously
2. All complaints are acknowledged within 24 hours of receipt
3. The complaint is logged immediately with the date, nature, and name of the complainant (if provided)
4. A full investigation is conducted within 7 business days
5. The client or family is informed of the resolution in writing within 10 business days
6. If the complaint cannot be resolved at the agency level, the client is informed of their right to contact the ISDH or IHCP
7. Retaliation against any person who files a complaint is prohibited and is grounds for immediate disciplinary action
8. All complaint documentation is retained for a minimum of 7 years

**Responsible Staff:** Any staff member (initial receipt), Supervisor (investigation), Administrator (resolution and documentation)

**Required Documentation:** Complaint log (date, nature, investigation steps, resolution, timeline), written resolution letter to client, retention for 7 years

# SECTION 7 — INFECTION CONTROL POLICY

**Policy Statement:**
Serene Comfort Home Care LLC follows strict infection control standards to prevent transmission of communicable diseases and protect the health and safety of clients and staff.

**Procedures:**
1. All caregivers perform hand hygiene (soap and water minimum 20 seconds, or hand sanitizer) before entering the client's home, before and after each care task, after removing gloves, and before leaving
2. Appropriate PPE (gloves, masks, gown as needed) is used for any task involving contact with blood, body fluids, or a client with a known communicable illness
3. PPE is discarded appropriately after each use; single-use items are never reused
4. Caregivers regularly disinfect all equipment and surfaces used during care per the care plan
5. Any caregiver with fever, respiratory symptoms, vomiting, diarrhea, or other signs of communicable illness must notify the supervisor immediately and is removed from the schedule until symptom-free per current CDC guidance
6. Any client with a suspected or confirmed communicable illness is documented; the care plan is updated with appropriate precautions and the supervisor is notified
7. All caregivers receive infection control training at orientation and annually
8. Exposure incidents are reported per Section F (OSHA & Bloodborne Pathogens)

**Responsible Staff:** All direct care staff, Supervisor (monitoring and care plan updates)

**Required Documentation:** Infection control training records, PPE supply log, client precaution documentation, illness-related schedule change records, exposure incident reports

# SECTION 13 — ABUSE PREVENTION AND REPORTING POLICY

**Policy Statement:**
Serene Comfort Home Care LLC has zero tolerance for abuse, neglect, exploitation, or mistreatment of any client in any form. All employees are mandatory reporters under Indiana law.

**Definitions:**
- **Abuse:** Intentional infliction of physical, emotional, or sexual harm
- **Neglect:** Failure to provide necessary care, supervision, or services
- **Exploitation:** Misuse of a client's property, finances, or authority over them
- **Mistreatment:** Any act or omission that causes unnecessary pain, distress, or harm

**Procedures:**
1. All staff receive training on recognizing and reporting abuse, neglect, and exploitation at orientation and annually
2. Any employee who suspects, witnesses, or is told about abuse, neglect, or exploitation must call the **Indiana Adult Protective Services (APS) Hotline: 1-800-992-6978** immediately — **do not delay to conduct an internal investigation first**
3. Staff notifies the Administrator within 2 hours of making the hotline call
4. If the Administrator is the alleged perpetrator, the report goes directly to the governing authority
5. Administrator preserves all related documentation and initiates an internal review
6. The alleged perpetrator is suspended from client contact pending investigation
7. If the allegation is substantiated, employment is terminated and the appropriate disclosures are made to ISDH and IHCP
8. Confidentiality of the victim and the reporting staff member is protected to the extent permitted by law
9. Retaliation against any person who makes a good-faith report is prohibited and is grounds for immediate termination

**Responsible Staff:** All staff — mandatory reporters

**Required Documentation:** APS report confirmation number, incident report, internal investigation record, corrective action plan, notification log

# SECTION 18 — UNSTABLE HEALTH CONDITION POLICY

**Policy Statement:**
When a client exhibits signs of an unstable or deteriorating health condition, caregivers respond promptly, notify appropriate parties, and document all actions taken. Client safety is the first priority.

**Procedures:**
1. Caregivers monitor clients for signs of health change during every visit, including: unusual pain, difficulty breathing, confusion or sudden change in mental status, inability to perform previously managed tasks, falls, or other concerning changes
2. If a client appears to be in immediate medical danger, the caregiver calls **911 first**, then notifies the supervisor
3. For non-emergency health changes, the caregiver notifies the supervisor **before the end of the shift**
4. Supervisor assesses the situation and determines next steps: care plan update, physician notification, emergency contact notification, or emergency response
5. The client's emergency contact and, if applicable, the client's physician or care coordinator are notified per the severity of the change
6. A Change-in-Condition report is completed within **24 hours**
7. The care plan is reviewed and amended if the change in condition requires a modification to authorized tasks
8. All actions taken are documented in the visit record and the change-in-condition report

**Responsible Staff:** Direct care staff (observation and reporting), Supervisor (assessment and coordination), Administrator (serious event escalation)

**Required Documentation:** Change-in-condition report, notification log (who was called, when, and what was communicated), care plan amendment if applicable, 911 call record if applicable

# SECTION 20 — TUBERCULOSIS TESTING POLICY

**Policy Statement:**
All employees must demonstrate freedom from active tuberculosis before providing client care. TB screening is required at hire and on an ongoing basis per current CDC and Indiana guidelines.

**Procedures:**
1. All new hires are required to provide a **negative TB test result** before their first day of client contact
2. Acceptable documentation: a negative tuberculin skin test (TST/PPD) administered within the past 12 months, a negative IGRA (QuantiFERON or T-SPOT) blood test, or a documented history of prior positive test with follow-up clearance from a licensed healthcare provider
3. Employees with a positive TB test result must provide written clearance from a licensed healthcare provider confirming no active TB disease before beginning client-facing work
4. Annual TB risk assessment is conducted for all employees; re-testing frequency follows current CDC guidelines for healthcare workers
5. Any employee who develops symptoms consistent with active TB (persistent cough, fever, night sweats, unexplained weight loss) is immediately removed from client contact and referred for medical evaluation
6. TB testing and screening records are maintained in the personnel file for the duration of employment plus 7 years

**Responsible Staff:** HR / Administrator (screening compliance), all employees (compliance)

**Required Documentation:** TB test result or equivalent clearance documentation in personnel file before first day of client contact, annual risk assessment records

# SUPPLEMENTAL SECTION C — BILLING, EVV & FINANCIAL COMPLIANCE

## C.1 Medicaid Billing Controls
Claims are submitted only for visits that were authorized, actually rendered, documented in a compliant visit note, and verified by a matching EVV record. Pre-submission review by billing staff and supervisor approval of the weekly claim batch are required before submission.

## C.2 Electronic Visit Verification (EVV)
EVV is required for every home care visit. Caregivers clock in and out via the approved EVV system at the client's service location. No visit is billable without a compliant EVV record. EVV exceptions are reviewed daily, reconciled weekly, and reported to the Administrator monthly.

## C.3 Overpayments
Any identified overpayment from IHCP is reported and returned within 60 days of identification. All overpayment records and refund confirmations are retained in the billing compliance file.

## C.4 Audit Cooperation
Serene Comfort Home Care LLC cooperates fully with all ISDH surveys, IHCP audits, and OIG reviews. Records are produced within required timeframes. Staff do not destroy, alter, or withhold records in connection with any audit or investigation.

# SUPPLEMENTAL SECTION D — DOCUMENTATION & TECHNOLOGY

## D.1 Electronic Documentation
All electronic systems used for client records or billing must be HIPAA-compliant, maintain an audit trail, and produce records accessible for regulatory review. Personal devices may only be used for agency business if enrolled in the agency's mobile device policy.

## D.2 Platform Access Separation
Elevate for Humanity (Curvature Body Sculpting LLC) staff access is limited to training records on all shared platforms. Client care records, billing data, and clinical documentation are accessible only to authorized Serene Comfort Home Care LLC staff. The Permission Matrix (Appendix K) governs all access assignments and is reviewed quarterly.

## D.3 HIPAA Security
All PHI transmitted electronically uses encrypted channels only. Audit logs from all PHI-bearing systems are exported and reviewed quarterly. Any suspected breach is investigated immediately and handled per the HIPAA Breach Notification Rule.

---

# SUPPLEMENTAL SECTION E — STAFFING: SCOPE, SCHEDULING & DISCIPLINE

## E.1 PCA Scope Limitation
Personal care aides assist with ADLs and IADLs as specified in the care plan only. PCAs do not perform skilled nursing tasks, administer medications, or provide services outside the care plan. Requests for out-of-scope tasks are referred to the supervisor — never performed unilaterally.

## E.2 Scheduling
Schedules are created to match authorized hours and service types. Authorized units are tracked; billing staff are alerted when 80% of authorized units in a period are consumed. Overtime requires Administrator approval.

## E.3 OIG Monthly Exclusion Check
All employees and active contractors are screened against the OIG LEIE on the first business day of every month. Results are logged. Any positive match results in immediate suspension from Medicaid-related activity pending investigation.

## E.4 Discipline
Disciplinary action is progressive: verbal warning → written warning → final written warning → termination. Immediate termination applies to: abuse, neglect, falsification of records, fraud, or any criminal conduct involving a client.

---

# SUPPLEMENTAL SECTION F — HIPAA, OSHA & MANDATORY REPORTING

## F.1 HIPAA
All client health information is confidential. Staff access only the minimum necessary PHI for their role. Unauthorized disclosure is a terminable offense. Notice of Privacy Practices is provided at intake. Business Associate Agreements are executed with all PHI-handling vendors.

## F.2 OSHA & Bloodborne Pathogens
A written Exposure Control Plan is maintained per 29 CFR 1910.1030. All direct care staff receive bloodborne pathogens training at orientation and annually. Any exposure incident is reported to the supervisor immediately; post-exposure evaluation follows the Exposure Control Plan.

## F.3 Mandatory Reporting
All employees are mandatory reporters. Suspected abuse, neglect, exploitation, or other reportable events are reported to the appropriate authority without internal delay. **APS Hotline: 1-800-992-6978. IHCP Fraud Hotline: 1-800-457-4515.**

## F.4 Fraud, Waste & Abuse
Zero-tolerance policy. FWA training at orientation and annually. Hotline information posted in the office and in the employee handbook. Confirmed FWA is reported to IHCP and appropriate authorities. Retaliation against good-faith reporters is prohibited.
