HVAC Technician Program Proposal — Latino Opportunity Center

Prepared for: Dulce Vega, Director of the Latino Opportunity Center | La Plaza, Inc.  |  March 2026

This proposal responds to the information requests from La Plaza's Latino Opportunity Center (LOC). It addresses all five areas outlined in your inquiry — Certification Pathways & Curriculum, Program Structure & Delivery, Student Support & Reporting, Career Outcomes & Employer Connectivity, and Financial Structure — for the HVAC Technician program as the recommended first cohort. A full list of additional programs offered by Elevate for Humanity is included at the end of this document.

1. Certification Pathway & Curriculum

Item Detail
Program HVAC Technician (newly launched program)
Total Duration 20 weeks (6–8 weeks classroom + 12 weeks OJT). Classroom phase structured Mon/Wed 5:30–8:30 PM to accommodate working adults.
Total Instructional Hours 400+ hours — 240 hours RTI (classroom/lab) + 160+ hours OJT (employer site)
Credentials Awarded EPA Section 608 Universal Certification
OSHA 10 Construction Safety
CPR / First Aid (American Heart Association)

State Approvals Indiana Workforce Ready Grant (WRG) Approved Provider   WIOA Title I Eligible Training Provider (ETPL Listed)
Proposed LOC Schedule Mondays & Wednesdays, 5:30 PM – 8:30 PM. Confirmed — this is our standard evening delivery model for working adults. Saturday sessions available if needed for accelerated completion.

Curriculum Competency Framework

Classroom / RTI Modules (240 hrs)

  • HVAC fundamentals — heating, cooling, refrigeration theory
  • Refrigerant types, handling, and EPA 608 exam prep
  • Electrical components — motors, controls, thermostats
  • Ductwork design and air distribution systems
  • Heat load calculations and system sizing
  • Troubleshooting and diagnostics
  • OSHA 10 safety — fall protection, PPE, hazard recognition
  • EPA 608 practice exams and certification
  • Workplace readiness and employer expectations
  • CPR / First Aid certification

OJT / Employer Site (160+ hrs)

  • Supervised installation — residential and light commercial
  • Preventive maintenance and service calls
  • Refrigerant recovery and recharge
  • Electrical troubleshooting on live systems
  • Ductwork installation and sealing
  • Customer interaction and job site professionalism
  • Employer evaluation and hour verification
  • DOL-compliant OJT documentation

2. Program Structure & Delivery

Item Detail
Cohort Size Minimum 15 participants / Maximum 30 participants
Modality Hybrid — RTI delivered online via LMS (accessible from any device). OJT completed at employer partner sites in Indianapolis / Marion County.
Schedule Mondays & Wednesdays, 5:30–8:30 PM (3 hrs/session, 6 hrs/week). Saturday sessions available for accelerated completion if needed.
Lab / Facility RTI: Online via LMS. OJT: Employer partner sites in Indianapolis, IN. Hands-on labs coordinated with employer partners in Marion County.
Equipment, Tools & PPE All included in program cost: safety glasses, gloves, hard hat, safety vest, program-specific hand tools, textbooks, study materials, and all certification exam fees.
Admission & Screening Online application → intake interview → eligibility verification → enrollment confirmation. LOC coaching team participates in intake screening to ensure alignment with participant goals and grant requirements.
Admission Requirements 18+ years old · HS diploma or GED · Valid driver's license · Ability to lift 50+ lbs · Pass background check · Basic math skills (fractions, measurements)
Bilingual Support Yes — bilingual instruction and Spanish-language materials available. Bilingual instructor coordination provided for LOC cohorts at no additional cost.

3. Student Support & Reporting

Item Detail
Tutoring & Academic Support On-demand tutoring through LMS, instructor office hours, and peer study groups. Bilingual academic support available for LOC cohorts.
Attendance Tracking LMS-tracked daily attendance with instructor sign-off. Real-time dashboard accessible to LOC coaching team.
Absence Alerts Automated alert to LOC case manager within 24 hours after 2 consecutive absences.
Progress Reports Bi-weekly progress reports during program. Monthly post-placement reports for 12 months after completion.
Report Format CSV and PDF export — Salesforce-compatible fields (participant ID, attendance %, module completion %, credential status, employment status). Custom field mapping available.
LOC Coaching Coordination Direct coordination with LOC coaching team for case management alignment. Shared participant dashboard access. Weekly status calls available upon request.
Wraparound Support Services Elevate for Humanity is currently in contract negotiations with the Indiana Family and Social Services Administration (FSSA). Upon finalization, this agreement may provide participating students with access to additional wraparound supports including transportation assistance and other barrier-removal services. We will keep LOC informed as this contract progresses.

4. Career Outcomes & Employer Connectivity

Item Detail
Work-Based Learning Elevate assists each participant in securing and completing their OJT component. We are actively developing employer partnerships in Marion County and will coordinate OJT placement directly for LOC cohort participants. OJT hours are supervised, documented, and verified through our LMS.
OJT Coordination We do not leave participants to find OJT on their own. Elevate works directly with each participant to identify, connect with, and confirm an OJT site. Pending employer partnerships in Marion County are being finalized ahead of the first cohort launch.
Starting Wage Range $18–$28/hr for HVAC graduates in the Indianapolis market — well above LOC's $14/hr target.
Job Placement Support Resume building, interview coaching, employer introductions, and direct referrals. Career services team coordinates placement for each cohort.
Retention Tracking 90-day and 180-day post-placement follow-up with employer wage verification. Employment status and wage data reported to LOC monthly.
Verified Work Hours OJT hours tracked via LMS timeclock and employer sign-off. Participants accumulate documented work experience hours toward Indiana HVAC apprenticeship registration.
LOC 2026 Outcome Targets — How We Align:

5. Financial Structure

Item Detail
Per-Participant Cost $2,700 per participant
What's Included All certification exam fees (EPA 608, OSHA 10, CPR/First Aid), PPE, tools, textbooks, LMS access, bilingual support, attendance reporting, and job placement assistance. No hidden costs.

Proposed Launch Timeline

1

Weeks 1–2 — Agreement & Intake

MOU / partnership agreement signed. LOC refers participants. Elevate conducts intake screening with LOC coaching team. Cohort size confirmed.

2

Weeks 3–4 — Enrollment & Orientation

Participants enrolled in LMS. Orientation completed (~2 hours, online). PPE and materials distributed. LOC coaching team granted dashboard access. First invoice issued.

3

Weeks 5–12 — Classroom Phase (RTI, Mon/Wed 5:30–8:30 PM)

6–8 weeks of evening instruction. Bi-weekly progress reports to LOC. Absence alerts within 24 hours. EPA 608 exam administered on-site at end of classroom phase.

4

Weeks 13–20 — OJT Phase

Participants placed at employer partner sites in Indianapolis. 160+ supervised hours. LOC coaching team receives weekly status updates. OJT hours documented for apprenticeship registration.

5

Week 20+ — Credential Issuance & Placement

All credentials issued. Job placement support activated. 90-day and 180-day follow-up tracking begins. Monthly employment reports delivered to LOC through 18-month outcome window.
