Program Detail — HVAC Technician

HVAC Technician — Curriculum Competency Framework

Classroom / RTI Modules (240 hrs)

  • HVAC fundamentals — heating, cooling, refrigeration theory
  • Refrigerant types, handling, and EPA 608 prep
  • Electrical components — motors, controls, thermostats
  • Ductwork design and air distribution
  • Heat load calculations and system sizing
  • Troubleshooting and diagnostics
  • OSHA 30 safety — fall protection, PPE, hazard recognition
  • EPA 608 exam preparation and practice tests
  • Workplace readiness and employer expectations

OJT / Employer Site (160+ hrs)

  • Supervised installation — residential and light commercial
  • Preventive maintenance and service calls
  • Refrigerant recovery and recharge
  • Electrical troubleshooting on live systems
  • Customer interaction and job site professionalism
  • Employer evaluation and hour verification

Admissions Requirements

  • 18+ years old | HS diploma or GED
  • Valid driver's license
  • Ability to lift 50+ lbs | Pass background check
  • Basic math skills (fractions, measurements)
Electrical Technician — Curriculum Competency Framework

Classroom / RTI Modules (240 hrs)

  • NEC (National Electrical Code) fundamentals
  • Residential wiring — circuits, panels, outlets, switches
  • Commercial wiring — conduit, 3-phase systems
  • Electrical theory — Ohm's Law, AC/DC circuits
  • Blueprint reading and electrical diagrams
  • OSHA 10 safety — electrical hazards, lockout/tagout
  • Indiana Electrical Apprentice Registration prep
  • Workplace readiness and employer expectations

OJT / Employer Site (160+ hrs)

  • Supervised residential and commercial wiring
  • Panel installation and circuit troubleshooting
  • Conduit bending and installation
  • Fixture and device installation
  • Employer evaluation and hour verification

Admissions Requirements

  • 18+ years old | HS diploma or GED
  • Basic algebra and fractions
  • Ability to distinguish wire colors
  • Ability to climb ladders | Pass background check
Plumbing Technician — Curriculum Competency Framework

Classroom / RTI Modules (240 hrs)

  • Indiana Plumbing Code fundamentals
  • Pipe materials — PVC, copper, PEX, cast iron
  • DWV (drain, waste, vent) system design
  • Water supply systems and pressure
  • Fixture installation — sinks, toilets, water heaters
  • Troubleshooting leaks, clogs, and pressure issues
  • OSHA 10 safety — trenching, confined spaces
  • Workplace readiness and employer expectations

OJT / Employer Site (160+ hrs)

  • Supervised residential and commercial plumbing
  • Pipe cutting, joining, and installation
  • Fixture installation and service
  • Drain cleaning and leak repair
  • Employer evaluation and hour verification

Admissions Requirements

  • 18+ years old | HS diploma or GED
  • Physical fitness — lift heavy materials, tight spaces
  • Basic math (measurements, slope calculations)
  • Pass background check
Forklift Operator Certification — Curriculum Competency Framework

Classroom (4 hrs)

  • OSHA 29 CFR 1910.178 regulations
  • Powered industrial truck types and capacities
  • Pre-operation inspection procedures
  • Load handling, stability, and center of gravity
  • Warehouse safety — pedestrian zones, speed limits
  • Written knowledge exam

Hands-On Evaluation (4 hrs)

  • Pre-trip inspection demonstration
  • Straight-line driving and turning
  • Load pickup, transport, and placement
  • Dock and ramp operation
  • Practical skills evaluation

Admissions Requirements

  • 18+ years old | Valid photo ID
  • Physical ability to operate powered industrial truck
  • No active restrictions on heavy equipment operation

2. Program Structure & Delivery

Item Detail
Cohort Size Minimum 15 participants / Maximum 30 participants per cohort
Modality Hybrid — Related Technical Instruction (RTI) delivered online via LMS. On-the-Job Training (OJT) completed as supervised internship at employer partner sites in Indianapolis / Marion County.
Proposed Schedule Mondays & Wednesdays, 5:30 PM – 8:30 PM (3 hrs/session, 6 hrs/week). Saturday sessions available if needed for accelerated completion.
Lab / Facility Location RTI: Online via LMS (accessible from any device). OJT: Employer partner sites in Indianapolis, IN (Marion County). Hands-on labs coordinated with employer partners.
Equipment, Tools & PPE All included in program cost: PPE (safety glasses, gloves, hard hat, safety vest), program-specific hand tools, textbooks, study materials, and certification exam fees.
Admission & Screening Online application → intake interview → eligibility verification → enrollment confirmation. LOC coaching team can participate in intake screening to ensure alignment with participant goals.
Bilingual Support Yes — bilingual instruction and language accessibility support available. Spanish-language materials and bilingual instructor coordination available for LOC cohorts.

3. Student Support & Reporting

Item Detail
Tutoring & Academic Support Available — on-demand tutoring through LMS, instructor office hours, and peer study groups. Bilingual academic support available for LOC cohorts.
Attendance Tracking LMS-tracked daily attendance with instructor sign-off. Real-time dashboard accessible to LOC coaching team.
Absence Alerts Automated alert to LOC case manager after 2 consecutive absences. Alert sent within 24 hours of missed session.
Progress Reports Bi-weekly progress reports during program. Monthly post-placement reports for 12 months after completion.
Report Format CSV and PDF export — Salesforce-compatible fields (participant ID, attendance %, module completion %, credential status, employment status). Custom field mapping available.
LOC Coaching Coordination Direct coordination with LOC coaching team for case management alignment. Shared participant dashboard access available. Weekly status calls available upon request.

4. Career Outcomes & Employer Connectivity

Item Detail
Work-Based Learning OJT internship at employer partner sites — supervised, paid or unpaid depending on employer agreement. Hours verified and documented for DOL Registered Apprenticeship compliance.
Employer Partnerships Active hiring pipelines with Indianapolis-area employers in HVAC, electrical, plumbing, and warehousing. Employer partners post open positions directly to our job board.
Job Placement Support Resume building, interview coaching, employer introductions, and direct referrals. Career services team coordinates placement for each cohort.
Retention Tracking 90-day and 180-day post-placement follow-up with employer verification. Employment status and wage data reported to LOC.
Verified Work Hours OJT hours tracked and verified through LMS timeclock and employer sign-off. Participants accumulate documented work experience hours toward apprenticeship registration.
LOC Outcome Targets We are aligned with your 2026 targets: 27 enrolled, 24 completing with credential, 20 employed at $14+/hr within 18 months. We will structure reporting to track against these specific benchmarks.
