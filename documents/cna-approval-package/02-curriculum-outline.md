## MODULE 1: Introduction to the CNA Role (6 hours)

**Learning Objectives:**

- Define the role and scope of practice of a CNA in Indiana
- Identify the members of the healthcare team and the CNA's position within it
- Describe the legal and ethical responsibilities of a CNA
- Explain HIPAA and patient confidentiality requirements
- Identify Indiana Nurse Aide Registry requirements

**Topics Covered:**

- History and evolution of the CNA profession
- Indiana Code 16-28-13 and 410 IAC 17 overview
- Scope of practice: what CNAs can and cannot do
- The interdisciplinary care team: RN, LPN, physician, therapist, social worker
- Chain of command and reporting structure
- Introduction to HIPAA: protected health information, verbal and written confidentiality
- Professional conduct, appearance, and attitude
- Introduction to the Indiana Nurse Aide Registry and competency exam

**Skills Introduced:** None (theory only)
**Assessment:** Module 1 written quiz (10 questions)

---

## MODULE 2: Infection Control & Standard Precautions (8 hours)

**Learning Objectives:**

- Explain the chain of infection and how to break it
- Demonstrate proper hand hygiene technique
- Apply standard precautions and transmission-based precautions correctly
- Don and doff personal protective equipment (PPE) properly
- Describe OSHA bloodborne pathogen standards

**Topics Covered:**

- The chain of infection: agent, reservoir, portal of exit, transmission, portal of entry, susceptible host
- Medical vs. surgical asepsis
- Hand hygiene: soap and water vs. alcohol-based hand rub, when each is required
- Standard precautions: gloves, gowns, masks, eye protection
- Transmission-based precautions: contact, droplet, airborne
- PPE donning and doffing sequence
- Bloodborne pathogens: HIV, Hepatitis B, Hepatitis C — exposure prevention and response
- Isolation procedures and signage
- Handling and disposal of contaminated materials
- Cleaning, disinfection, and sterilization concepts

**Skills Lab:** Hand hygiene, PPE donning/doffing
**Assessment:** Module 2 written quiz (10 questions)

---

## MODULE 3: Safety, Body Mechanics & Emergency Procedures (7 hours)

**Learning Objectives:**

- Apply correct body mechanics to prevent injury during patient care
- Identify and respond to common safety hazards in healthcare settings
- Demonstrate proper use of side rails, call lights, and bed positioning
- Describe fire safety procedures (RACE/PASS)
- Respond appropriately to a patient fall, choking, or cardiac emergency

**Topics Covered:**

- Principles of body mechanics: alignment, balance, base of support
- Ergonomics in patient care: lifting, pushing, pulling
- Environmental safety: wet floors, clutter, lighting, call light placement
- Restraint alternatives and restraint use regulations
- Fall prevention: risk factors, bed alarms, non-slip footwear, bed height
- Fire safety: RACE (Rescue, Alarm, Contain, Extinguish), PASS (Pull, Aim, Squeeze, Sweep)
- Disaster and emergency preparedness
- Responding to a fall: what to do, what NOT to do, documentation
- Choking response: conscious and unconscious adult
- Basic CPR awareness (CNAs must know when to call for help and initiate CPR if trained)
- Reporting incidents and completing incident reports

**Skills Lab:** Body mechanics practice, fall response simulation
**Assessment:** Module 3 written quiz (10 questions)

---

## MODULE 4: Patient Rights, Ethics & Communication (6 hours)

**Learning Objectives:**

- Identify the Resident Rights guaranteed under federal and Indiana law
- Recognize and report abuse, neglect, and exploitation
- Demonstrate therapeutic communication techniques
- Adapt communication for patients with hearing, vision, or cognitive impairments

**Topics Covered:**

- Federal Nursing Home Reform Act (OBRA 1987) and Resident Rights
- Indiana patient rights in non-nursing home settings
- Right to privacy, dignity, self-determination, and grievance
- Abuse, neglect, and exploitation: definitions, types, mandatory reporting
- Indiana Adult Protective Services reporting requirements
- Therapeutic communication: active listening, open-ended questions, non-verbal cues
- Barriers to communication: language, culture, hearing loss, dementia
- Communicating with patients who have aphasia, dementia, or anxiety
- Cultural competency and person-centered care
- Advance directives: DNR, living will, healthcare proxy — CNA's role

**Skills Introduced:** Communication role-play exercises
**Assessment:** Module 4 written quiz (10 questions)

---

## MODULE 5: Vital Signs (8 hours)

**Learning Objectives:**

- Accurately measure oral, axillary, and tympanic temperature
- Measure radial and apical pulse
- Count respiratory rate
- Measure blood pressure using manual and electronic methods
- Measure and record height and weight
- Identify normal ranges and when to report abnormal findings

**Topics Covered:**

- Definition and purpose of vital signs
- Temperature: normal ranges, types of thermometers, measurement sites, technique
- Pulse: radial vs. apical, rate, rhythm, strength — normal range 60–100 bpm
- Respiration: rate, depth, rhythm — normal range 12–20 breaths/min
- Blood pressure: systolic vs. diastolic, normal range, hypertension, hypotension
- Sphygmomanometer use: manual auscultation technique
- Electronic vital sign equipment operation
- Oxygen saturation (SpO2): pulse oximetry basics
- Height and weight measurement: standing scale, bed scale
- Pain as the fifth vital sign: numeric and visual pain scales
- Documentation: accurate recording, military time, abbreviations
- When and how to report abnormal findings to the charge nurse

**Skills Lab:** Temperature, pulse, respiration, blood pressure, height/weight
**Assessment:** Module 5 written quiz (15 questions) + skills demonstration

---

## MODULE 6: Personal Care Skills (12 hours)

**Learning Objectives:**

- Perform complete bed bath, partial bath, and shower/tub assist
- Provide oral hygiene including denture care
- Assist with dressing and grooming while preserving dignity
- Perform perineal care for male and female patients
- Make an occupied and unoccupied bed

**Topics Covered:**

- Principles of personal care: privacy, dignity, patient preference, infection control
- Complete bed bath: sequence, water temperature, technique, draping
- Partial bath and self-care assistance
- Shower and tub bath assistance: safety, transfer, supervision
- Oral hygiene: brushing teeth, denture care, mouth care for unconscious patients
- Hair care: brushing, combing, shampooing in bed
- Nail care: fingernails (CNAs do not cut toenails of diabetic patients)
- Shaving: electric and safety razor technique
- Dressing and undressing: weak side first, strong side first rule
- Perineal care: female and male technique, catheter care
- Bed making: unoccupied bed, occupied bed, mitered corners
- Skin inspection during care: pressure injury staging (Stage I–IV), reporting

**Skills Lab:** Bed bath, oral hygiene, bed making, perineal care, dressing assist
**Assessment:** Module 6 written quiz (15 questions) + skills demonstration

---

## MODULE 7: Mobility, Transfers & Positioning (10 hours)

**Learning Objectives:**

- Position patients in Fowler's, semi-Fowler's, lateral, prone, and Sims' positions
- Transfer a patient from bed to wheelchair using proper technique and gait belt
- Assist with ambulation using gait belt and assistive devices
- Operate a mechanical lift safely
- Apply and remove anti-embolism stockings

**Topics Covered:**

- Importance of mobility: preventing contractures, pressure injuries, pneumonia, DVT
- Positioning: supine, prone, lateral (side-lying), Fowler's, semi-Fowler's, Sims'
- Pressure injury prevention: repositioning schedule (every 2 hours), pressure-relieving devices
- Gait belt: application, grip, use during transfers and ambulation
- Bed to wheelchair transfer: stand-pivot technique, two-person assist
- Wheelchair to bed transfer
- Mechanical lift operation: sling application, lift operation, safety checks
- Ambulation assistance: walker, cane, crutches — CNA support role
- Range of motion (ROM) exercises: passive ROM, purpose, technique for each joint
- Anti-embolism stockings: application, removal, skin checks
- Contracture prevention and restorative care principles

**Skills Lab:** Positioning, gait belt transfer, mechanical lift, ROM exercises
**Assessment:** Module 7 written quiz (15 questions) + skills demonstration

---

## MODULE 8: Nutrition, Hydration & Elimination (7 hours)

**Learning Objectives:**

- Assist patients with meals while maintaining dignity and safety
- Monitor and record fluid intake and output (I&O)
- Provide care for patients with urinary catheters
- Assist with bedpan, urinal, and commode use
- Recognize signs of dehydration and malnutrition

**Topics Covered:**

- Basic nutrition: macronutrients, therapeutic diets (low-sodium, diabetic, pureed, thickened liquids)
- Dysphagia: signs, aspiration risk, positioning during meals, thickened liquids
- Meal assistance: setup, feeding technique, pacing, documentation
- Fluid intake and output: measurement, recording, normal output ranges
- Dehydration: signs, symptoms, prevention, reporting
- Urinary elimination: normal patterns, urinary incontinence, catheter care
- Indwelling catheter care: perineal care, tubing management, bag positioning, signs of UTI
- Bowel elimination: constipation, diarrhea, fecal impaction — recognition and reporting
- Bedpan, urinal, and commode assistance: technique, privacy, documentation
- Ostomy awareness: types, bag care basics, skin protection

**Skills Lab:** Meal assist, I&O measurement, catheter care, bedpan assist
**Assessment:** Module 8 written quiz (10 questions)

---

## MODULE 9: Dementia, Mental Health & Restorative Care (7 hours)

**Learning Objectives:**

- Describe the stages and symptoms of Alzheimer's disease and related dementias
- Apply person-centered dementia care techniques
- Recognize common mental health conditions in the long-term care population
- Support patients with depression, anxiety, and behavioral symptoms
- Describe restorative nursing principles

**Topics Covered:**

- Dementia overview: Alzheimer's, vascular dementia, Lewy body, frontotemporal
- Stages of Alzheimer's: early, middle, late — behavioral and functional changes
- Person-centered dementia care: validation therapy, redirection, reminiscence
- Sundowning: causes, prevention, response strategies
- Wandering: safety measures, elopement prevention
- Behavioral symptoms: agitation, aggression, repetitive behaviors — de-escalation techniques
- Depression in older adults: signs, CNA's role in observation and reporting
- Anxiety and PTSD in long-term care residents
- Psychotropic medications: CNA awareness, side effects to monitor
- Restorative care: ADL retraining, independence promotion, documentation
- Reality orientation vs. validation therapy

**Skills Lab:** Dementia communication role-play, restorative ADL practice
**Assessment:** Module 9 written quiz (10 questions)

---

## MODULE 10: Chronic Conditions & End-of-Life Care (6 hours)

**Learning Objectives:**

- Identify common chronic conditions in the CNA's patient population
- Provide appropriate care for patients with diabetes, COPD, CHF, and stroke
- Describe the stages of grief and dying
- Provide compassionate end-of-life care
- Support families during the dying process

**Topics Covered:**

- Diabetes: types, hypoglycemia vs. hyperglycemia signs, foot care, diet monitoring
- COPD: oxygen use, positioning, activity tolerance, breathing techniques
- Congestive heart failure: fluid restrictions, edema monitoring, daily weights
- Stroke/CVA: hemiplegia, aphasia, dysphagia, ADL adaptations
- Arthritis and musculoskeletal conditions: ROM, pain management, assistive devices
- Cancer in long-term care: pain management, comfort care, dignity
- Kübler-Ross stages of grief: denial, anger, bargaining, depression, acceptance
- Hospice and palliative care: philosophy, CNA's role, comfort measures
- Signs of approaching death: Cheyne-Stokes breathing, mottling, decreased urine output
- Post-mortem care: procedure, dignity, family support
- Supporting families: presence, communication, grief resources

**Assessment:** Module 10 written quiz (10 questions)

---

## MODULE 11: Documentation & Reporting (5 hours)

**Learning Objectives:**

- Document patient care accurately using approved abbreviations
- Complete an Activities of Daily Living (ADL) flow sheet
- Identify what must be reported immediately to the charge nurse
- Describe the minimum data set (MDS) and the CNA's contribution

**Topics Covered:**

- Principles of documentation: accuracy, timeliness, objectivity, legibility
- Medical abbreviations and terminology used in CNA documentation
- ADL flow sheets: purpose, completion, common errors
- Narrative charting: SOAP and DAR formats — awareness level
- Electronic health records (EHR): CNA access and documentation responsibilities
- What to report immediately: falls, changes in condition, refusal of care, abuse
- Incident reports: purpose, completion, confidentiality
- Minimum Data Set (MDS): what it is, how CNA observations contribute
- HIPAA in documentation: who can access records, verbal reporting precautions
- Change-of-shift report: what to include, how to give a concise report

**Assessment:** Module 11 written quiz (10 questions)

---

## MODULE 12: Clinical Preparation & Competency Review (4 hours)

**Learning Objectives:**

- Demonstrate all required skills at competency level before clinical rotation
- Identify expectations and professional conduct standards for clinical sites
- Review Indiana Nurse Aide Competency Evaluation format and requirements

**Topics Covered:**

- Review of all skills: hand hygiene, vital signs, transfers, personal care, bed making
- Skills competency check-off: each student must pass all required skills
- Clinical site expectations: dress code, conduct, supervision, documentation
- Indiana Nurse Aide Competency Evaluation: written exam format, skills exam format
- Test-taking strategies for the written exam
- Scheduling the competency exam through Headmaster Testing Services
- Indiana Nurse Aide Registry: how to verify listing, renewal requirements
- Career pathways after CNA: LPN bridge, home health, hospital employment

**Skills Lab:** Full competency review and mock skills exam
**Assessment:** Final written exam (50 questions) + skills competency check-off
