# CNA PROGRAM — COMPLIANCE LANGUAGE AND DISCLOSURES

## Elevate for Humanity LLC

---

## SECTION 1: PROGRAM APPROVAL STATUS DISCLOSURE

Elevate for Humanity LLC is currently seeking approval from the Indiana State Department of Health (ISDH) to operate a Certified Nursing Assistant (CNA) training program under Indiana Code 16-28-13 and 410 IAC 17.

**This program has not yet received state approval.** Elevate for Humanity LLC will not enroll students in the CNA program, collect tuition or fees for the CNA program, or represent the CNA program as approved until written approval is received from the Indiana State Department of Health.

All marketing materials, website content, and student communications will clearly state "pending ISDH approval" until official written approval is granted. Upon receipt of approval, all materials will be updated to reflect approved status.

---

## SECTION 2: CERTIFICATION AND LICENSURE DISCLOSURE

Completion of the Elevate for Humanity LLC CNA training program does not, by itself, confer certification, licensure, or any professional credential.

Upon successful completion of all program requirements, graduates are **eligible** to sit for the Indiana Nurse Aide Competency Evaluation administered by Headmaster Testing Services on behalf of the Indiana State Department of Health. Passage of the competency evaluation — including both the written examination and the skills demonstration — is required for listing on the Indiana Nurse Aide Registry.

Elevate for Humanity LLC does not guarantee that any student will pass the Indiana Nurse Aide Competency Evaluation or obtain employment following program completion.

The term "CNA" or "Certified Nursing Assistant" in program materials refers to the training program and the credential for which graduates become eligible — not to a credential conferred by Elevate for Humanity LLC.

---

## SECTION 3: EMPLOYMENT DISCLOSURE

Elevate for Humanity LLC does not guarantee employment to any program graduate. Employment outcomes depend on individual student performance, local labor market conditions, employer hiring decisions, and passage of the Indiana Nurse Aide Competency Evaluation.

Salary and wage information provided in program materials represents median or typical ranges based on publicly available data from the Bureau of Labor Statistics and Indiana Department of Workforce Development. Actual wages vary by employer, location, experience, and shift differential.

---

## SECTION 4: REFUND AND CANCELLATION POLICY

**If the program does not receive ISDH approval:** Students who have submitted enrollment applications or deposits prior to program approval will receive a full refund of any fees paid within 30 days of the determination that approval will not be granted.

**If a student withdraws before the program begins:** Full refund of any fees paid, minus a non-refundable application processing fee of $25.

**If a student withdraws after the program begins:**

- Withdrawal before completing 10% of program hours: 90% refund of tuition paid
- Withdrawal after completing 10% but before 25% of program hours: 75% refund
- Withdrawal after completing 25% but before 50% of program hours: 50% refund
- Withdrawal after completing 50% or more of program hours: No refund

Refund requests must be submitted in writing to the program director. Refunds will be processed within 30 days of the written withdrawal request.

**WIOA and grant-funded students:** Refund policies for students funded through WIOA, Workforce Ready Grant, JRI, or other public funding sources are governed by the applicable funding agency's policies. Elevate for Humanity LLC will comply with all applicable refund requirements.

## SECTION 5: NON-DISCRIMINATION POLICY

Elevate for Humanity LLC does not discriminate on the basis of race, color, national origin, sex, disability, age, religion, sexual orientation, gender identity, veteran status, or any other characteristic protected by federal, state, or local law in its admissions, programs, activities, or employment practices.

This policy applies to all aspects of the program including enrollment, instruction, clinical placement, and program completion.

Students who believe they have been subjected to discrimination may file a complaint with the program director using the grievance process described in the Student Policies and Handbook. Students may also file a complaint with the U.S. Department of Education Office for Civil Rights or the Indiana Civil Rights Commission.

## SECTION 6: STUDENT RECORDS AND PRIVACY

Elevate for Humanity LLC maintains student records in accordance with applicable federal and state privacy laws. Student records are kept confidential and are not disclosed to third parties without the student's written consent, except as required by law or as necessary for program administration (e.g., clinical site verification of student enrollment status).

Students have the right to review their own program records upon written request to the program director.

## SECTION 7: REQUIRED REGULATORY LANGUAGE FOR MARKETING MATERIALS

All marketing materials, website content, social media posts, and student communications related to the CNA program must include the following language until ISDH approval is received:

_"The Certified Nursing Assistant (CNA) program at Elevate for Humanity LLC is pending approval by the Indiana State Department of Health. Enrollment is not open until approval is granted. Joining the waiting list does not constitute enrollment and does not guarantee a seat in any cohort."_

After ISDH approval is received, marketing materials must include:

_"The Certified Nursing Assistant (CNA) program at Elevate for Humanity LLC is approved by the Indiana State Department of Health under Indiana Code 16-28-13 and 410 IAC 17. Completion of this program makes graduates eligible to sit for the Indiana Nurse Aide Competency Evaluation. Completion does not guarantee passage of the examination or employment."_
