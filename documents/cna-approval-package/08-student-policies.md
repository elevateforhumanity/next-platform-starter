# CNA PROGRAM — STUDENT POLICIES AND HANDBOOK

## Elevate for Humanity LLC

**Effective Date:** Upon program approval
**Applies To:** All students enrolled in the Certified Nursing Assistant (CNA) program

---

## SECTION 1: ATTENDANCE POLICY

Regular attendance is essential for student success and is required for program completion and eligibility for the Indiana Nurse Aide Competency Evaluation.

**Classroom and Skills Lab:**
Students are expected to attend all scheduled classroom and skills laboratory sessions. A student who misses more than 10% of total classroom/lab hours (more than 8 hours of the 80 classroom/lab hours) will be placed on attendance probation. A student who misses more than 20% of total classroom/lab hours (more than 16 hours) will be dismissed from the program and must reapply for the next cohort.

**Clinical:**
Clinical attendance is mandatory. All 25 clinical hours must be completed. There are no excused absences from clinical that do not require makeup. Students who miss a clinical day must notify the program director before the scheduled start time. Makeup hours are subject to clinical site availability and are not guaranteed. Students who cannot complete makeup hours within the program timeline will not be eligible for the competency examination.

**Tardiness:**
Arriving more than 10 minutes late to a classroom, lab, or clinical session counts as a tardy. Three tardies equal one absence for attendance calculation purposes. Chronic tardiness may result in dismissal from the program at the program director's discretion.

**Notification:**
Students must notify the program director or instructor by phone or email before the start of any missed session. Failure to notify without a documented emergency will result in an unexcused absence.

---

## SECTION 2: GRADING POLICY

**Written Assessments:**
Students must achieve a minimum score of 75% on each written module quiz and a minimum score of 75% on the final written examination to pass the program.

Students who score below 75% on a module quiz may retake the quiz once. The retake score replaces the original score. Students who score below 75% on the retake will receive remediation instruction and may retake the quiz a second time. Students who do not achieve 75% after two retakes will be placed on academic probation.

**Skills Assessments:**
Students must demonstrate satisfactory performance on all required skills. Each skill may be attempted a maximum of three times. Students who do not achieve satisfactory performance after three attempts will be dismissed from the program.

**Final Grade:**
A passing final grade requires: (1) minimum 75% on all written assessments, (2) satisfactory performance on all skills, and (3) completion of all clinical hours with a satisfactory clinical evaluation.

**Grading Scale:**

- 90–100%: A
- 80–89%: B
- 75–79%: C (minimum passing)
- Below 75%: F (failing — remediation required)

---

## SECTION 3: DRESS CODE AND APPEARANCE

Students must adhere to the following dress code during all classroom, skills lab, and clinical sessions:

- Solid-color scrubs (top and bottom) — no patterns, logos, or graphics
- Closed-toe, closed-heel, non-slip shoes — no sandals, clogs, or open-toe shoes
- Hair must be clean and secured away from the face — no hair hanging below the collar during patient care
- Nails must be short, clean, and free of nail polish or artificial nails — artificial nails harbor bacteria and are prohibited in clinical settings
- Jewelry limited to a plain wedding band and small stud earrings — no dangling earrings, bracelets, or necklaces
- No strong perfume or cologne — many patients have respiratory sensitivities
- Student identification badge must be worn and visible at all times
- Tattoos must be covered if visible and potentially offensive to patients

Students who arrive to clinical in violation of the dress code will be sent home and the session will count as an absence.

---

## SECTION 4: CONDUCT POLICY

Students are expected to conduct themselves professionally at all times. The following behaviors are grounds for immediate dismissal from the program:

- Physical, verbal, or emotional abuse of any patient, classmate, instructor, or staff member
- Theft or misappropriation of any property
- Falsification of records, documentation, or attendance
- Reporting to class or clinical under the influence of alcohol or drugs
- Possession of alcohol, illegal drugs, or weapons on program premises or clinical sites
- Violation of patient confidentiality (including social media posts)
- Cheating or academic dishonesty
- Insubordination or refusal to follow reasonable instructor directives
- Sexual harassment or discrimination

For conduct issues that do not rise to the level of immediate dismissal, the following progressive discipline process applies: verbal warning → written warning → final written warning → dismissal.

---

## SECTION 5: ACADEMIC INTEGRITY

Students are expected to complete all assignments, quizzes, and examinations independently and honestly. Academic dishonesty includes: copying from another student's exam, using unauthorized materials during an exam, submitting another person's work as your own, and falsifying clinical documentation.

Students found to have engaged in academic dishonesty will receive a zero on the affected assessment and will be placed on academic probation. A second offense will result in dismissal from the program.

---

## SECTION 6: SKILLS PASS/FAIL REQUIREMENTS

All required skills must be performed at a satisfactory level as defined in the Skills Competency Checklist. Critical steps (identified in the checklist) must be performed correctly on every attempt. Failure to perform a critical step results in an automatic unsatisfactory rating for that attempt.

Students have a maximum of three attempts per skill. Students who do not achieve satisfactory performance after three attempts will be dismissed from the program. The program director may, at their discretion, authorize additional remediation and a fourth attempt in exceptional circumstances.

---

## SECTION 7: CLINICAL PARTICIPATION REQUIREMENTS

To participate in clinical training, students must:

1. Have completed all classroom and skills laboratory components
2. Have passed all required skills at satisfactory level
3. Have a current negative tuberculosis (TB) test result (within 12 months) or chest X-ray
4. Have current CPR certification (Heartsaver or BLS level)
5. Have completed a criminal background check (results reviewed by program director)
6. Have completed a drug screening (if required by the clinical site)
7. Have signed the Clinical Participation Agreement
8. Have completed HIPAA training acknowledgment

Students who do not meet all clinical participation requirements by the scheduled clinical start date will not be permitted to begin clinical training and may be withdrawn from the program.

---

## SECTION 8: DISMISSAL CRITERIA

Students may be dismissed from the program for any of the following:

- Exceeding the maximum allowable absences
- Failure to achieve passing grades after remediation
- Failure to pass required skills after three attempts
- Conduct violations as described in Section 4
- Academic dishonesty as described in Section 5
- Failure to meet clinical participation requirements
- Substantiated finding of patient abuse, neglect, or exploitation

Students who are dismissed from the program will receive written notification from the program director. Dismissed students may reapply for the next available cohort, subject to program director approval.

---

## SECTION 9: COMPLAINT AND GRIEVANCE PROCESS

Students who have a complaint or grievance related to the program, an instructor, or a clinical experience should follow this process:

**Step 1 — Informal Resolution:** Discuss the concern directly with the instructor or program director within five business days of the incident.

**Step 2 — Formal Written Complaint:** If the concern is not resolved informally, the student may submit a written complaint to the program director within ten business days of the incident. The program director will respond in writing within ten business days.

**Step 3 — Appeal:** If the student is not satisfied with the program director's response, they may appeal in writing to the organization's executive director within ten business days of receiving the program director's response. The executive director's decision is final.

Students will not be retaliated against for filing a good-faith complaint or grievance.
