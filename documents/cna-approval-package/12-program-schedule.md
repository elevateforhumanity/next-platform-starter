## DAILY SESSION STRUCTURE

**Standard Classroom Day (6 hours):**

- 8:00–8:15 AM: Attendance, warm-up, review of previous session
- 8:15–10:00 AM: New content instruction (105 minutes)
- 10:00–10:15 AM: Break
- 10:15–12:00 PM: Continued instruction or skills demonstration (105 minutes)
- 12:00–12:30 PM: Lunch break
- 12:30–1:45 PM: Skills practice or case study review (75 minutes)
- 1:45–2:00 PM: Quiz, Q&A, session wrap-up (15 minutes)

**Clinical Day (8–9 hours):**

- Arrive at clinical site 15 minutes before scheduled start
- Pre-clinical briefing with clinical instructor (15 minutes)
- Patient care assignments and supervised skills practice
- Mid-day check-in with clinical instructor
- Post-clinical debrief and daily log completion (30 minutes)

## COMPETENCY EXAMINATION SCHEDULING

Upon successful completion of all program requirements, students will be registered for the Indiana Nurse Aide Competency Evaluation administered by Headmaster Testing Services. The program director will assist students with scheduling. The competency examination is typically available within 2–4 weeks of program completion.

Students must pass both the written examination (70 questions) and the skills demonstration (5 skills) to be listed on the Indiana Nurse Aide Registry.
