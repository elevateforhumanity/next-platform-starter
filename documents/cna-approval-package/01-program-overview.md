## PROGRAM DESCRIPTION

The Certified Nursing Assistant (CNA) program at Elevate for Humanity LLC is a structured, competency-based training program designed to prepare students for entry-level employment as certified nursing assistants in Indiana's healthcare sector. The program meets and exceeds the minimum training requirements established by the Indiana State Department of Health (ISDH) under Indiana Code 16-28-13 and 410 IAC 17.

Students receive instruction in foundational nursing assistant theory, hands-on skills laboratory practice, and supervised clinical experience in a licensed long-term care or healthcare facility. Upon successful completion of all program requirements, graduates are eligible to sit for the Indiana Nurse Aide Competency Evaluation administered by Headmaster Testing Services.

The program is designed to serve adult learners, career changers, justice-involved individuals, WIOA-eligible participants, and individuals seeking entry into the healthcare workforce. No prior healthcare experience is required for enrollment.

## TOTAL PROGRAM HOURS

Indiana NATCEP Administrative Standards require a minimum of 75 total training hours: at least 30 hours of classroom instruction and at least 45 hours of clinical training. Of the 45 clinical hours, up to 21 hours may be conducted in a skills laboratory; a minimum of 24 hours must be completed in a licensed long-term care facility under direct RN supervision at a ratio not to exceed 8:1.

Elevate for Humanity LLC meets these requirements as follows:

| Component                                   | Indiana Minimum  | This Program |
| ------------------------------------------- | ---------------- | ------------ |
| Classroom / Theory Instruction              | 30 hours         | 30 hours     |
| Skills Laboratory (part of clinical bucket) | up to 21 hours   | 21 hours     |
| Licensed LTC Facility Clinical              | 24 hours minimum | 24 hours     |
| **Total Clinical (Lab + LTC combined)**     | **45 hours**     | **45 hours** |
| **Total Program Hours**                     | **75 hours**     | **75 hours** |

**Important distinctions:**

- Skills laboratory hours are counted within the 45-hour clinical requirement, not as a separate third category.
- The 21 lab hours are conducted at Elevate for Humanity LLC's on-site skills lab, 8888 Keystone Crossing, Suite 1300, Indianapolis, IN 46240.
- The 24 LTC facility hours are conducted at a licensed long-term care facility under a signed clinical affiliation agreement, under direct supervision of a qualified RN instructor.
- No training of any kind will begin until written program approval is received from the Indiana State Department of Health (IDOH).

## PROGRAM OBJECTIVES

Upon completion of this program, students will be able to:

1. Demonstrate knowledge of the role, responsibilities, and legal scope of practice of a Certified Nursing Assistant in Indiana.
2. Apply principles of infection control, standard precautions, and personal protective equipment use in clinical settings.
3. Perform personal care skills including bathing, grooming, oral hygiene, dressing, and toileting with respect for patient dignity and privacy.
4. Accurately measure and record vital signs including temperature, pulse, respiration, and blood pressure.
5. Safely assist patients with mobility, transfers, positioning, and ambulation using proper body mechanics and assistive devices.
6. Provide nutritional support including meal assistance, fluid intake monitoring, and documentation.
7. Communicate effectively with patients, families, and members of the interdisciplinary healthcare team.
8. Recognize and respond appropriately to changes in patient condition, including emergency situations.
9. Apply knowledge of patient rights, HIPAA, and ethical standards in all aspects of care.
10. Demonstrate competency in all required skills as evaluated by a qualified RN instructor.
