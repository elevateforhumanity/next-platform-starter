# CNA PROGRAM — CLINICAL TRAINING PLAN

## Elevate for Humanity LLC

---

## OVERVIEW

Clinical training is a required component of the Certified Nursing Assistant program at Elevate for Humanity LLC. Clinical experience provides students with the opportunity to apply classroom knowledge and skills laboratory competencies in a real healthcare setting under the direct supervision of a qualified Registered Nurse clinical instructor.

No student will participate in clinical training until they have successfully completed all classroom modules and demonstrated competency in all required skills in the skills laboratory.

---

## CLINICAL HOURS

Indiana NATCEP Administrative Standards require a minimum of 45 total clinical training hours. Of those 45 hours:

- Up to 21 hours may be conducted in a skills laboratory setting.
- A minimum of 24 hours must be completed in a licensed long-term care (LTC) facility under direct RN supervision.

Elevate for Humanity LLC meets these requirements exactly:

| Component                   | Indiana Requirement    | This Program |
| --------------------------- | ---------------------- | ------------ |
| Skills Laboratory hours     | Up to 21 hours (max)   | 21 hours     |
| Licensed LTC Facility hours | 24 hours (minimum)     | 24 hours     |
| **Total Clinical Hours**    | **45 hours (minimum)** | **45 hours** |

Skills laboratory hours are conducted at Elevate for Humanity LLC's on-site skills lab, 8888 Keystone Crossing, Suite 1300, Indianapolis, IN 46240.

LTC facility hours are conducted at a licensed long-term care facility under a signed clinical affiliation agreement. Students must complete all 45 clinical hours (21 lab + 24 LTC) to be eligible for the Indiana Nurse Aide Competency Evaluation.

**No clinical training of any kind will begin until written program approval is received from IDOH.**

---

## CLINICAL SITE REQUIREMENTS

Clinical training will be conducted at a licensed long-term care facility, skilled nursing facility, or other approved healthcare setting in Indiana. The clinical site must:

- Hold a current Indiana license in good standing
- Be free of any federal or state enforcement actions that would prohibit student training
- Have a signed clinical affiliation agreement with Elevate for Humanity LLC on file
- Provide adequate patient census to allow students to perform required skills
- Designate a facility contact person to coordinate student placement

Elevate for Humanity LLC will maintain a current list of approved clinical sites and will provide students with their assigned site at least one week before the clinical rotation begins.

---

## STUDENT-TO-INSTRUCTOR RATIO

The maximum student-to-instructor ratio during clinical training is **8 students to 1 RN instructor**, in compliance with Indiana requirements under 410 IAC 17.

The clinical instructor is a Registered Nurse (RN) licensed in Indiana with a minimum of two years of nursing experience, at least one year of which is in long-term care or a related setting. The instructor must hold current CPR certification.

The clinical instructor will be physically present at the clinical site at all times during student training. Students will not be left unsupervised with patients under any circumstances.

---

## SUPERVISION STRUCTURE

**Direct Supervision:** The RN clinical instructor directly observes student performance of all skills during the first clinical day. The instructor provides immediate feedback and corrects technique in real time.

**Indirect Supervision:** After demonstrating competency on the first clinical day, students may perform routine care tasks with the instructor available on the unit. The instructor checks in with each student at minimum every 30 minutes and is immediately available if the student has a question or concern.

**Escalation Protocol:** If a student encounters a situation beyond their training or comfort level, they are instructed to stop, maintain patient safety, and immediately notify the clinical instructor. Students are never expected to manage a clinical situation independently.

---

## SKILLS TO BE PERFORMED DURING CLINICAL

Students are expected to perform the following skills during their clinical rotation, as patient assignments and census allow:

**Personal Care Skills:**

- Complete or partial bed bath
- Oral hygiene (natural teeth and/or dentures)
- Hair care and grooming
- Dressing and undressing assistance
- Perineal care
- Bed making (occupied and unoccupied)

**Vital Signs:**

- Oral, axillary, or tympanic temperature
- Radial pulse
- Respiratory rate
- Blood pressure (manual or electronic)
- Oxygen saturation (pulse oximetry)
- Height and weight

**Mobility and Safety:**

- Positioning and repositioning
- Gait belt application
- Transfer (bed to wheelchair or wheelchair to bed)
- Ambulation assistance
- Range of motion exercises (passive)

**Nutrition and Elimination:**

- Meal assistance
- Fluid intake and output measurement and recording
- Bedpan or commode assistance

**Documentation:**

- ADL flow sheet completion
- Vital signs documentation
- Reporting observations to the charge nurse

Students are NOT permitted to perform the following during clinical: medication administration, IV insertion or management, catheter insertion or removal, wound care beyond basic skin care, or any procedure not covered in the training program.

---

## CLINICAL EVALUATION METHOD

Student performance during clinical is evaluated by the RN clinical instructor using the **Clinical Performance Evaluation Form** (see Forms section). The evaluation assesses:

- Safety and infection control practices
- Technical skill performance
- Communication with patients, families, and staff
- Professional conduct and appearance
- Documentation accuracy
- Response to supervision and feedback

**Scoring:** Each competency area is rated as Satisfactory (S), Needs Improvement (NI), or Unsatisfactory (U).

**Passing Standard:** Students must receive a Satisfactory rating in all critical competency areas to pass the clinical rotation. Students who receive an Unsatisfactory rating in any critical area will be counseled by the clinical instructor and program director. A remediation plan will be developed. Students who do not achieve Satisfactory performance after remediation will not be cleared for the competency examination.

---

## DOCUMENTATION PROCESS

**Daily Clinical Log:** Each student completes a daily clinical log documenting the skills performed, the patient population served (without identifying information), and a brief reflection on their learning. The log is reviewed and signed by the clinical instructor at the end of each clinical day.

**Clinical Performance Evaluation Form:** Completed by the clinical instructor at the end of the clinical rotation. A copy is provided to the student and retained in the student's program file.

**Incident Documentation:** Any incident involving a student during clinical (patient fall, exposure, injury, or complaint) is documented on an incident report form, reported to the program director, and handled in accordance with facility and program policies.

**Attendance:** Clinical attendance is mandatory. Students who miss a clinical day must make up the hours before being eligible for the competency examination. Makeup clinical hours are subject to site availability and are not guaranteed.

---

## CLINICAL CONDUCT STANDARDS

Students are expected to adhere to the following standards during all clinical experiences:

- Arrive on time in proper uniform (solid-color scrubs, closed-toe non-slip shoes, hair secured, no visible jewelry except small stud earrings, no artificial nails)
- Wear student identification badge at all times
- Follow all facility policies and procedures
- Maintain patient confidentiality — no photos, no social media posts, no discussion of patients outside the facility
- Address all patients by their preferred name and title
- Report all observations and concerns to the clinical instructor immediately
- Refrain from use of personal cell phones during patient care
- Treat all facility staff, patients, and families with respect

Violations of clinical conduct standards may result in removal from the clinical site and dismissal from the program.
