# CNA PROGRAM — INSTRUCTOR AND FACILITY REQUIREMENTS

## Elevate for Humanity LLC

---

## SECTION 1: INSTRUCTOR QUALIFICATIONS

### Primary Classroom and Skills Lab Instructor

The primary instructor responsible for classroom instruction and skills laboratory supervision must meet all of the following requirements:

**Licensure:**

- Current, unrestricted Registered Nurse (RN) license issued by the Indiana Professional Licensing Agency (IPLA)
- License must be in good standing with no disciplinary actions, restrictions, or pending investigations

**Experience:**

- Minimum of two (2) years of nursing experience as a licensed RN
- Minimum of one (1) year of experience in long-term care, skilled nursing, or a related patient care setting
- Experience in teaching, training, or staff development preferred

**Certifications:**

- Current CPR certification at the BLS (Basic Life Support) or Heartsaver level from the American Heart Association or American Red Cross
- Completion of a train-the-trainer or nurse aide instructor course, or documented equivalent teaching experience

**Background:**

- Clear criminal background check (no disqualifying offenses)
- Clear Indiana Nurse Aide Registry — not listed as having a substantiated finding of abuse, neglect, or exploitation
- Clear OIG (Office of Inspector General) exclusion list check

**Ongoing Requirements:**

- Maintain current RN licensure throughout employment
- Complete continuing education as required by the Indiana State Nurses Association and IPLA
- Participate in annual program review and curriculum updates

---

### Clinical Instructor

The clinical instructor supervises students during clinical rotations at approved healthcare facilities. The clinical instructor must meet all of the following requirements:

**Licensure:**

- Current, unrestricted Registered Nurse (RN) license issued by the Indiana Professional Licensing Agency (IPLA)

**Experience:**

- Minimum of two (2) years of nursing experience
- Minimum of one (1) year of experience in long-term care or the clinical setting where supervision occurs

**Certifications:**

- Current CPR certification (BLS or Heartsaver level)

**Ratio:**

- Maximum of eight (8) students per clinical instructor during all clinical rotations
- The clinical instructor must be physically present at the clinical site at all times during student training

**Responsibilities:**

- Orient students to the clinical facility before patient contact
- Directly observe student skill performance
- Provide immediate corrective feedback
- Complete the Clinical Performance Evaluation Form for each student
- Report any incidents, injuries, or conduct concerns to the program director immediately
- Maintain communication with the facility's charge nurse throughout the clinical day

---

### Program Director

The program director is responsible for overall program administration, compliance, and quality assurance.

**Qualifications:**

- Minimum of a bachelor's degree in nursing, healthcare administration, education, or a related field, OR documented equivalent experience in healthcare program management
- Familiarity with Indiana CNA training requirements under 410 IAC 17
- Administrative experience in a healthcare or educational setting

**Responsibilities:**

- Oversee curriculum development and updates
- Hire and supervise instructors
- Maintain program records and student files
- Ensure compliance with all ISDH requirements
- Manage clinical site agreements
- Respond to student complaints and grievances
- Submit required reports to the Indiana State Department of Health
- Conduct annual program evaluation

---

## SECTION 2: INSTRUCTOR TEACHING RESPONSIBILITIES

The primary instructor is responsible for:

1. Delivering all classroom instruction in accordance with the approved curriculum
2. Supervising all skills laboratory sessions and evaluating student skill performance
3. Maintaining accurate attendance records for all sessions
4. Administering and grading all written assessments
5. Providing academic counseling and remediation to students who are not meeting standards
6. Maintaining a safe and respectful learning environment
7. Reporting any student conduct concerns to the program director
8. Completing all required documentation including skills competency check-offs and grade records
9. Participating in program evaluation and curriculum review
10. Maintaining current knowledge of Indiana CNA training requirements and competency exam content

---

## SECTION 3: FACILITY AND EQUIPMENT REQUIREMENTS

### Classroom Requirements

The classroom used for CNA theory instruction must meet the following minimum requirements:

- Sufficient seating for all enrolled students (maximum class size: 16 students)
- Adequate lighting for reading and writing
- Climate control (heating and air conditioning)
- Whiteboard or equivalent display surface
- Projector or large-screen display for multimedia instruction
- Internet access for online learning resources
- Accessible restroom facilities
- Compliance with all applicable fire, safety, and accessibility codes
- ADA-compliant access for students with disabilities

### Skills Laboratory Requirements

**Space:**

- Minimum 400 square feet of usable floor space

## SECTION 4: RECORD KEEPING REQUIREMENTS

Elevate for Humanity LLC will maintain the following records for a minimum of five (5) years:

- Student enrollment records (application, enrollment agreement, demographic information)
- Attendance records for all classroom, lab, and clinical sessions
- Skills competency check-off forms for each student
- Written assessment scores and final grades
- Clinical performance evaluation forms
- Incident reports
- Instructor credentials and licensure verification
- Clinical site affiliation agreements
- Program approval documentation from ISDH
- Annual program evaluation reports

Records will be stored securely and made available to the Indiana State Department of Health upon request.
