**1.** Which of the following is the MOST effective action a CNA can take to prevent the spread of infection?

A) Wearing a gown at all times
B) Performing hand hygiene before and after patient contact
C) Keeping the patient's door closed
D) Wearing gloves for all patient interactions

**2.** A patient has been placed on contact precautions for MRSA. Which PPE is required when entering the room?

A) N95 respirator only
B) Surgical mask and gloves
C) Gown and gloves
D) Gown, gloves, and N95 respirator

**3.** When performing hand hygiene with soap and water, the CNA should scrub for a minimum of:

A) 5 seconds
B) 10 seconds
C) 20 seconds
D) 60 seconds

**4.** A CNA is caring for a patient with C. difficile (C. diff). Which hand hygiene method is required?

A) Alcohol-based hand rub only
B) Soap and water only
C) Either soap and water or alcohol-based hand rub
D) No special precautions are needed

**5.** Which of the following is the correct sequence for removing (doffing) PPE?

A) Mask → gown → gloves → hand hygiene
B) Gloves → gown → mask → hand hygiene
C) Gown → gloves → mask → hand hygiene
D) Gloves → mask → gown → hand hygiene

**6.** A patient tells a CNA, "I don't want a bath today." The CNA should:

A) Explain that the bath is required and proceed
B) Respect the patient's right to refuse and report the refusal to the charge nurse
C) Ask the patient's family to convince them
D) Document that the patient is non-compliant

**7.** Under OBRA 1987, residents of nursing facilities have the right to:

A) Choose their own physician only if they pay privately
B) Be free from abuse, neglect, and exploitation
C) Refuse all medications without consequence
D) Have unlimited visitors at any hour

**8.** A CNA witnesses a coworker yelling at a patient and calling them names. The CNA should:

A) Ignore it — it is not their responsibility
B) Tell the coworker to stop and report the incident to the charge nurse immediately
C) Wait to see if it happens again before reporting
D) Ask the patient if they want to file a complaint

**9.** Which of the following is an example of patient neglect?

A) Accidentally spilling water on a patient
B) Leaving a patient in a soiled brief for three hours
C) Forgetting to bring a patient their requested snack
D) Being late to answer a call light by five minutes

**10.** A family member asks a CNA what diagnosis their loved one has been given. The CNA should:

A) Share the diagnosis since the person is family
B) Refer the family member to the charge nurse or physician
C) Tell the family member to check the patient's chart
D) Explain the diagnosis in general terms only

**11.** The chain of infection includes six links. Which action breaks the "mode of transmission" link?

A) Treating the infectious agent with antibiotics
B) Isolating the susceptible host
C) Wearing gloves and a gown during contact precautions
D) Improving the patient's nutrition to boost immunity

**12.** A CNA is about to perform perineal care and realizes they forgot to bring gloves into the room. The CNA should:

A) Perform the care quickly without gloves
B) Use the patient's washcloth as a barrier
C) Leave the patient, perform hand hygiene, get gloves, and return
D) Ask the patient to wait until the next shift

**13.** Which of the following is NOT within the CNA's scope of practice?

A) Measuring and recording vital signs
B) Administering oral medications
C) Assisting with ambulation using a gait belt
D) Performing passive range of motion exercises

**14.** A patient's advance directive states "Do Not Resuscitate" (DNR). The patient stops breathing. The CNA should:

A) Begin CPR immediately
B) Call for the nurse immediately and do not begin CPR
C) Ask the family what they want done
D) Begin CPR until the nurse arrives

**15.** The Indiana Nurse Aide Registry requires CNAs to work a minimum of how many hours of paid nursing-related work every 24 months to maintain active status?

A) 4 hours
B) 8 hours
C) 12 hours
D) 16 hours

### EXAM 1 ANSWER KEY

| Q   | Answer | Rationale                                                                        |
| --- | ------ | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1   | B      | Hand hygiene is the single most effective infection prevention measure           |
| 2   | C      | Contact precautions require gown and gloves                                      |
| 3   | C      | Minimum 20 seconds of scrubbing required                                         |
| 4   | B      | Alcohol does not kill C. diff spores — soap and water required                   |
| 5   | B      | Gloves first (most contaminated), then gown, then mask                           |
| 6   | B      | Residents have the right to refuse care; refusal must be documented and reported |
| 7   | B      | OBRA 1987 guarantees freedom from abuse, neglect, and exploitation               |
| 8   | B      | Verbal abuse must be stopped and reported immediately                            |
| 9   | B      | Leaving a patient in a soiled brief for extended time is neglect                 |
| 10  | B      | HIPAA requires referral to the appropriate licensed provider                     |
| 11  | C      | PPE interrupts the mode of transmission link                                     |
| 12  | C      | Never perform care without appropriate PPE                                       |
| 13  | B      | Medication administration is outside CNA scope of practice                       |
| 14  | B      | CNA must call for nurse immediately and honor the DNR                            |
| 15  | B      | 8 hours every 24 months required per Indiana registry rules                      |

**1.** The normal range for adult blood pressure is:

A) Less than 90/60 mmHg
B) Less than 120/80 mmHg
C) 130–140/80–90 mmHg
D) 140/90 mmHg or higher

**2.** A CNA measures a patient's radial pulse and finds it is 112 beats per minute and irregular. The CNA should:

A) Recount the pulse and document the finding
B) Report the finding to the charge nurse immediately
C) Wait until the end of the shift to report
D) Assume the patient was just exercising

**3.** When measuring blood pressure, the cuff should be placed:

A) Over the patient's clothing for comfort
B) On the arm with an IV line for accuracy
C) On the bare upper arm, 1 inch above the antecubital fossa
D) As tightly as possible to get an accurate reading

**4.** Normal oxygen saturation (SpO2) for a healthy adult is:

A) 85–90%
B) 90–94%
C) 95–100%
D) 100% only

**5.** A patient's oral temperature is 101.2°F. The CNA should:

A) Recheck in one hour and document
B) Apply a cool cloth and recheck
C) Report the finding to the charge nurse immediately
D) Offer the patient extra fluids and document

**6.** When performing a complete bed bath, the CNA should wash body areas in which order?

A) Feet → legs → abdomen → chest → face
B) Face → arms → chest → abdomen → legs → back → perineum
C) Perineum → back → legs → chest → face
D) Any order is acceptable as long as all areas are washed

**7.** During a bed bath, the CNA notices a reddened area over the patient's sacrum that does not turn white when pressed. The CNA should:

A) Apply lotion and continue the bath
B) Document the finding at the end of the shift
C) Report the finding to the charge nurse immediately
D) Cover the area with a bandage

**8.** When assisting a patient to dress, the CNA should:

A) Dress the stronger side first
B) Dress the weaker side first
C) Ask the patient which side they prefer
D) It does not matter which side is dressed first

**9.** A gait belt should be applied:

A) Over bare skin for a secure grip
B) Over the patient's clothing, snugly around the waist
C) Around the patient's chest for better control
D) As loosely as possible to avoid discomfort

**10.** When performing a stand-pivot transfer from bed to wheelchair, the wheelchair should be positioned:

A) Directly in front of the bed
B) At a 90-degree angle to the bed
C) At a 45-degree angle to the bed on the patient's stronger side
D) At a 45-degree angle to the bed on the patient's weaker side

**11.** A patient begins to fall during ambulation. The CNA should:

A) Try to hold the patient up to prevent the fall
B) Call for help and let the patient fall
C) Guide the patient to the floor by bending the CNA's knees and lowering them gently
D) Pull the patient backward toward the wall

**12.** Passive range of motion exercises are performed to:

A) Strengthen the patient's muscles
B) Prevent contractures and maintain joint flexibility
C) Prepare the patient for discharge
D) Reduce the patient's blood pressure

**13.** When repositioning a patient in bed, the CNA should change the patient's position at minimum every:

A) 30 minutes
B) 1 hour
C) 2 hours
D) 4 hours

**14.** Which of the following is a Stage 2 pressure injury?

A) Non-blanchable redness of intact skin
B) Partial thickness skin loss — shallow open ulcer or blister
C) Full thickness skin loss with subcutaneous tissue visible
D) Full thickness tissue loss with bone visible

**15.** Before using a mechanical lift to transfer a patient, the CNA must:

A) Obtain the patient's verbal consent only
B) Check that the sling is the correct size and properly positioned, and that the lift is functioning
C) Remove the patient's clothing for better sling contact
D) Inflate the lift to maximum height before positioning the patient

**1.** A patient with dysphagia is ordered nectar-thick liquids. The CNA brings the patient a glass of water. This is:

A) Acceptable if the patient requests it
B) A violation of the patient's diet order that could cause aspiration
C) Fine as long as the patient drinks slowly
D) Only a concern if the patient has a history of pneumonia

**2.** Normal urine output for an adult is approximately:

A) 10 mL per hour
B) 30 mL per hour
C) 100 mL per hour
D) 200 mL per hour

**3.** A patient with CHF gains 3 pounds overnight. The CNA should:

A) Encourage the patient to drink more fluids
B) Document the weight and report to the charge nurse immediately
C) Reweigh the patient in the afternoon for accuracy
D) Assume the scale is inaccurate and use a different scale

**4.** A patient with dementia says, "I need to go home to feed my cats." The best response using validation therapy is:

A) "You don't have cats anymore. You live here now."
B) "You can't go home. This is your home."
C) "It sounds like you love your cats. Tell me about them."
D) "Let's talk about something else."

**5.** Sundowning in patients with dementia refers to:

A) Increased confusion and agitation in the late afternoon and evening
B) Sleeping excessively during the day
C) Refusing meals at dinnertime
D) Wandering outside the facility

**6.** Signs of hypoglycemia (low blood sugar) in a diabetic patient include:

A) Increased thirst, frequent urination, and fruity breath
B) Shakiness, sweating, confusion, and pale skin
C) Swelling in the legs and shortness of breath
D) Fever, chills, and increased blood pressure

**7.** When caring for a patient with COPD, the CNA should position the patient in:

A) Supine (flat on back)
B) Prone (face down)
C) Fowler's or semi-Fowler's (head elevated)
D) Left lateral (side-lying)

**8.** A patient who had a stroke has left-sided weakness (hemiplegia). When assisting with ambulation, the CNA should walk:

A) On the patient's left (weaker) side
B) On the patient's right (stronger) side
C) Directly behind the patient
D) In front of the patient facing them

**9.** Which of the following is an objective documentation statement?

A) "Patient appears to be in pain."
B) "Patient seems confused today."
C) "Patient's skin is red and warm to touch over the sacrum, approximately 2 cm in diameter."
D) "Patient has a pressure sore on their back."

**10.** A CNA should report which of the following to the charge nurse immediately?

A) A patient who ate 75% of their meal
B) A patient who has not produced urine in 5 hours
C) A patient who requested an extra blanket
D) A patient who fell asleep during a television program

**11.** Signs of approaching death include all of the following EXCEPT:

A) Cheyne-Stokes respiration
B) Mottling of the skin
C) Increased urine output
D) Decreased responsiveness

**12.** Post-mortem care refers to:

A) Care provided to a patient who is expected to die soon
B) Care provided to the body after death has been pronounced
C) Emotional support provided to the family after a death
D) Documentation completed after a patient is discharged

**13.** The Minimum Data Set (MDS) is:

A) A list of minimum equipment required in a nursing facility
B) A federal assessment tool used to evaluate resident functional status in nursing facilities
C) A form completed by the CNA to request supplies
D) A record of the minimum number of staff required per shift

**14.** When correcting a documentation error, the CNA should:

A) Use correction fluid to cover the error and write the correct information
B) Scribble out the error so it cannot be read
C) Draw a single line through the error, write "error," initial it, and write the correct information
D) Tear out the page and start over

**15.** A patient in hospice care is unresponsive. The CNA should:

A) Stop providing personal care since the patient cannot respond
B) Continue all personal care with the same dignity and respect, speaking to the patient as if they can hear
C) Limit care to essential tasks only and avoid disturbing the patient
D) Ask the family to perform personal care

### EXAM 3 ANSWER KEY

| Q   | Answer | Rationale                                                                                                |
| --- | ------ | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1   | B      | Giving thin liquids to a patient ordered thickened liquids is a diet order violation and aspiration risk |
| 2   | B      | Normal urine output is approximately 30 mL/hour                                                          |
| 3   | B      | Weight gain of 2+ lbs in 24 hours in CHF requires immediate reporting                                    |
| 4   | C      | Validation therapy acknowledges the emotion, not the factual content                                     |
| 5   | A      | Sundowning = increased confusion/agitation in late afternoon/evening                                     |
| 6   | B      | Shakiness, sweating, confusion, pale skin = hypoglycemia                                                 |
| 7   | C      | Fowler's/semi-Fowler's maximizes lung expansion for COPD patients                                        |
| 8   | A      | Walk on the weaker side to provide support where it is needed                                            |
| 9   | C      | Objective documentation describes observable facts without interpretation                                |
| 10  | B      | No urine output for 4+ hours requires immediate reporting                                                |
| 11  | C      | Urine output DECREASES (not increases) as death approaches                                               |
| 12  | B      | Post-mortem care = care of the body after death is pronounced                                            |
| 13  | B      | MDS is a federal resident assessment tool used in nursing facilities                                     |
| 14  | C      | Single line, "error," initials, correct information — never use correction fluid                         |
| 15  | B      | Continue full dignified care — hearing may persist; speak to the patient                                 |
