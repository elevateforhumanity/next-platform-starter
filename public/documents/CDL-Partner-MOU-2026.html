I. Purpose of Agreement

This Memorandum of Understanding ("MOU") is entered into between Elevate for Humanity Career & Technical Institute ("Elevate," "Training Provider") and the Partner identified above ("Partner," "Employer," "Agency," or "Funding Sponsor"). This MOU outlines the goals, objectives, program structure, costs, payment terms, and mutual expectations governing enrollment of one or more students in the C1 CDL Training Program.

By executing this agreement, the Partner confirms that each enrolled student has demonstrated the responsibility and willingness to uphold all requirements herein, and the Partner assumes financial responsibility on behalf of the student(s) receiving instruction and training.

II. Program Goals & Objectives
Class A CDL Program

To provide students with the knowledge, skills, and hands-on driving experience required to successfully obtain a Class A Commercial Driver's License and begin a career as a professional commercial driver.

Class B CDL Program

To equip students with the competencies necessary to obtain a Class B Commercial Driver's License and enter the workforce as a qualified commercial vehicle operator.

Overall Program Goals
  • Deliver high-quality classroom, range, and road instruction in compliance with federal and state CDL standards and FMCSA ELDT requirements.
  • Prepare each student for the CDL skills test.
  • Support students through employment preparation and job placement assistance to ensure strong career outcomes.
  • Maintain compliance with all applicable DOL, FMCSA, and Indiana BMV regulations.
III. Program Costs

IV. Payment Terms & Invoicing

Elevate for Humanity is responsible for managing all funding, invoicing, and payment collection. Partners and employers must invoice Elevate directly. The following payment schedule applies to all employer-sponsored, agency-funded, and partner-referred enrollments:

Payment Schedule — Per Student
Invoice 1 — Program Start (50%)
Due upon confirmed class start date. Training does not begin until Invoice 1 is received. 50% of tuition
Invoice 2 — Program Completion (50%)
Due upon student completion of all required training hours and skills test attempt. 50% of tuition
Referral Partner Incentive: Funding referral partners who refer a student that successfully starts a class will receive $500 per student class start. Referral fees are paid by Elevate for Humanity within 30 days of confirmed class start. Referral partners must be identified in writing prior to enrollment to qualify.
Late Payment: Invoices unpaid within 30 days of the due date are subject to a 1.5% monthly late fee. Elevate reserves the right to suspend future enrollments for partners with outstanding balances.
V. Program Format & Structure
Classroom Training — Both Class A & Class B
  • Duration: One (1) week
  • Delivery: Online or in-person
  • Curriculum: FMCSA ELDT theory, safety regulations, foundational CDL knowledge
Behind-the-Wheel, Range & Road Training
  • Class A CDL: Three (3) weeks of range and road instruction, followed by CDL skills test
  • Class B CDL: One (1) week of range and road instruction, followed by CDL skills test
On-the-Job Training (OJT)

Upon successful completion of the CDL skills test and issuance of a Commercial Driver's License, students may be placed in an On-the-Job Training (OJT) arrangement with a partner employer. OJT placements are coordinated by Elevate's Job Placement Coordinator. The Partner/Employer is responsible for providing a safe, compliant OJT work environment and for supervising the student during the OJT period in accordance with applicable DOL and FMCSA standards.

VI. Attendance & Conduct Expectations
Attendance
  • Students may have no more than two (2) absences during the program.
  • Exceeding the maximum absence limit may result in dismissal from the program.
Training Hours
  • Monday – Friday, 7:00 a.m. – 4:00 p.m.
  • Includes one (1) hour lunch and two (2) fifteen-minute breaks.
  • Students must arrive on time each day. Excessive tardiness may result in disciplinary action or removal.
Transportation Notice: Transportation to and from CDL training each day must be provided by the student, employer, or agency funding the training. Elevate for Humanity does not provide transportation services.
Professional Conduct

Students are expected to comply with all facility rules, safety guidelines, and instructor direction at all times. Violations may result in immediate dismissal without refund.

VII. Job Placement & Career Support
  • Each student will be assigned a Job Placement Coordinator who will provide guidance on employment opportunities and carrier partnerships.
  • Students will be introduced to job opportunities beginning on Day 1 of the program.
  • Representatives from partner carriers will regularly present to classes throughout training.
  • The Partner/Employer is responsible for providing OJT supervision and a compliant work environment for any student placed with their organization.
VIII. Drop & Dismissal Policy

A student may be dismissed from the program for any of the following reasons:

  1. Failure to meet attendance or timeliness requirements.
  2. Insufficient progress or inability to meet skill requirements during training.
  3. Failure or refusal of required drug screening.
  4. Violation of safety rules or conduct standards, as determined by program leadership.

In the event of dismissal, the Partner/Employer remains responsible for the 50% program start payment already invoiced. The completion payment (Invoice 2) will not be due if the student does not complete the program.

IX. Student Records & Elevate Rights
  • Student Records: Elevate for Humanity retains all rights to student training records, completion documentation, credential records, and program data in accordance with FERPA and applicable workforce program requirements.
  • Credential Ownership: CDL credentials are issued by the Indiana BMV directly to the student. Elevate retains records of training completion and skills test preparation.
  • Program Intellectual Property: All curriculum, training materials, assessments, and program content developed by Elevate for Humanity are the sole property of Elevate for Humanity and may not be reproduced, distributed, or used without written consent.
X. Confidentiality, Non-Disclosure & Non-Compete
Non-Disclosure

Both parties agree to keep confidential all proprietary information, student data, pricing, curriculum, business processes, and partner relationships disclosed in connection with this MOU. Neither party shall disclose such information to any third party without prior written consent, except as required by law.

Non-Compete

During the term of this MOU and for a period of two (2) years following its termination, the Partner agrees not to directly solicit, recruit, or hire Elevate for Humanity instructors, staff, or subcontractors for the purpose of replicating or competing with Elevate's CDL training program. The Partner further agrees not to use Elevate's curriculum, training materials, or program structure to establish a competing CDL training program without written authorization from Elevate for Humanity.

Student Non-Solicitation

The Partner agrees not to directly solicit students enrolled in or referred through Elevate's program for competing training programs during the term of this MOU and for one (1) year following termination.

XI. Term & Termination
  • This MOU is effective upon the date of last signature below and remains in effect for one (1) year, automatically renewing annually unless terminated in writing by either party with 30 days' notice.
  • Either party may terminate this MOU for cause with written notice. Outstanding payment obligations survive termination.

The Partner confirms that each enrolled student has demonstrated the responsibility and willingness to uphold the requirements outlined in this document, and the Partner assumes full financial responsibility on behalf of the student(s) receiving instruction and training.

This MOU constitutes the entire agreement between the parties with respect to its subject matter and supersedes all prior discussions, representations, or agreements.
