# Electrical Technician

## Program Syllabus

**Provider:** Elevate for Humanity  
**ETPL Provider ID:** 10000949  
**Program Code:** ELEC-TECH  
**Duration:** 16-24 Weeks (640-960 Hours)  
**Delivery Method:** Classroom + Field Work  
**Funding:** Registered Apprenticeship • WIOA • WRG

---

## Program Overview

Start your career in the electrical trades! Learn residential and commercial wiring, electrical code, circuit design, troubleshooting, and safety procedures. Program combines classroom theory with real-world installations. Graduate ready for apprenticeship positions or immediate employment with electrical contractors.

---

## Learning Objectives

1. Install residential and commercial electrical systems
2. Apply National Electrical Code (NEC) requirements
3. Read and interpret electrical blueprints
4. Troubleshoot electrical circuits and systems
5. Use electrical testing equipment safely
6. Perform conduit bending and installation
7. Wire control circuits and motor starters
8. Follow OSHA electrical safety standards

---

## Course Outline

### Module 1: Electrical Safety & Fundamentals (60 hours)

- OSHA 10-Hour Construction Safety
- Electrical hazards and safety
- Personal protective equipment
- Lockout/tagout procedures
- Arc flash awareness
- First aid for electrical injuries
- Basic electrical theory

### Module 2: Electrical Theory (80 hours)

- Voltage, current, and resistance
- Ohm's Law and power calculations
- Series and parallel circuits
- AC and DC theory
- Inductance and capacitance
- Transformers and motors
- Three-phase systems

### Module 3: National Electrical Code (NEC) (60 hours)

- NEC structure and navigation
- Wiring methods and materials
- Branch circuits and feeders
- Grounding and bonding
- Overcurrent protection
- Special occupancies
- Code calculations

### Module 4: Residential Wiring (120 hours)

- Service entrance installation
- Panel installation and wiring
- Branch circuit wiring
- Receptacle and switch installation
- Lighting circuits
- GFCI and AFCI protection
- Low-voltage systems (doorbell, thermostat)

### Module 5: Commercial Wiring (120 hours)

- Commercial service equipment
- Conduit systems (EMT, rigid, PVC)
- Wire pulling techniques
- Commercial lighting systems
- Emergency and exit lighting
- Fire alarm systems basics
- Motor circuits

### Module 6: Conduit Bending & Installation (60 hours)

- Hand bender techniques
- Offset bends
- Three-point saddles
- Parallel offsets
- Conduit supports and fittings
- Hydraulic bender operation

### Module 7: Motor Controls (60 hours)

- Motor types and applications
- Motor starters and contactors
- Overload protection
- Control circuits
- Variable frequency drives (VFD) basics
- Troubleshooting motor circuits

### Module 8: Blueprint Reading (40 hours)

- Reading electrical drawings
- Symbols and abbreviations
- One-line diagrams
- Panel schedules
- Lighting and power plans
- Control diagrams

### Module 9: Electrical Testing & Troubleshooting (60 hours)

- Multimeter usage
- Continuity and resistance testing
- Voltage and current measurement
- Insulation testing
- Circuit tracing
- Troubleshooting techniques

### Module 10: Field Training (200-400 hours)

- Supervised installation work
- Residential and commercial projects
- Tool proficiency development
- Code compliance verification
- Customer interaction
- Professional conduct
