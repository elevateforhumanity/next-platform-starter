# Culinary Arts & Food Service

## Program Syllabus

**Provider:** Elevate for Humanity  
**ETPL Provider ID:** 10000949  
**Program Code:** CULINARY  
**Duration:** 12-18 Months (1,500-2,000 Hours)  
**Delivery Method:** Registered Apprenticeship (Earn While You Learn)  
**Funding:** Registered Apprenticeship • WIOA • WRG

---

## Program Overview

Turn your passion for cooking into a career through Registered Apprenticeship! Earn while you learn in professional kitchens. Master food preparation, kitchen safety, menu planning, nutrition, and restaurant operations through hands-on training. Graduate ready for line cook, prep cook, or food service management positions in restaurants, hotels, and catering.

---

## Learning Objectives

1. Prepare food using professional cooking techniques
2. Apply food safety and sanitation standards
3. Use commercial kitchen equipment safely
4. Execute recipes and maintain consistency
5. Perform knife skills and food preparation
6. Understand nutrition and dietary requirements
7. Manage kitchen operations and inventory
8. Work effectively in fast-paced kitchen environment

---

## Course Outline

### Phase 1: Foundations (Months 1-4, 500 Hours)

#### Classroom Instruction (100 hours)

- Food safety and sanitation (ServSafe)
- Kitchen safety and OSHA standards
- Knife skills and equipment use
- Culinary terminology
- Recipe reading and measurement
- Nutrition basics
- Professional conduct

#### On-the-Job Training (400 hours)

- Kitchen orientation and safety
- Basic food preparation
- Vegetable and fruit preparation
- Stock and soup preparation
- Sanitation procedures
- Equipment operation
- Station setup and breakdown

### Phase 2: Core Skills (Months 5-10, 750 Hours)

#### Classroom Instruction (100 hours)

- Cooking methods and techniques
- Meat, poultry, and seafood preparation
- Sauce preparation
- Baking fundamentals
- Menu planning
- Cost control and inventory
- Customer service

#### On-the-Job Training (650 hours)

- Sauté, grill, and roast techniques
- Protein preparation and cooking
- Pasta and grain dishes
- Salad and cold food preparation
- Plating and presentation
- Line cook responsibilities
- Speed and efficiency development

### Phase 3: Advanced & Specialization (Months 11-18, 750 Hours)

#### Classroom Instruction (100 hours)

- Advanced cooking techniques
- International cuisines
- Dietary restrictions and allergies
- Kitchen management
- Food costing and ordering
- Leadership and supervision
- Career development

#### On-the-Job Training (650 hours)

- Lead line cook responsibilities
- Menu development and specials
- Training junior staff
- Inventory management
- Quality control
- Opening and closing procedures
- Full kitchen operations

---

## Required Hours Breakdown

**Total Program Hours:** 1,500-2,000 (DOL Registered Apprenticeship)

- **Classroom/Related Instruction:** 300 hours minimum
- **On-the-Job Training:** 1,200-1,700 hours
- **Practical Competencies:**
  - Food preparation techniques
  - Cooking methods mastery
  - Kitchen equipment operation
  - Sanitation and safety compliance

---

## Assessment Methods

- Monthly progress reports from chef/supervisor
- Classroom exams (70% passing)
- Practical cooking demonstrations
- ServSafe certification exam
- Recipe execution evaluations
- Speed and accuracy assessments
- Final comprehensive evaluation

---

## Grading Scale

- **Exceeds Standards:** Advanced culinary skills, ready for lead positions
- **Meets Standards:** Competent performance, on track
- **Needs Improvement:** Additional practice required
- **Unsatisfactory:** Does not meet minimum standards

---

## Required Materials

- Chef knife set (provided or stipend available)
- Chef coat and pants (provided)
- Non-slip kitchen shoes (required)
- Thermometer and basic tools (provided)
- ServSafe study materials (provided)

---

## Instructor/Mentor Qualifications

**Classroom Instructors:**

- Culinary degree or equivalent experience
- ServSafe Instructor certification
- Minimum 5 years professional cooking experience

**On-the-Job Mentors:**

- Executive Chef or Sous Chef
- Minimum 5 years professional kitchen experience
- Approved by DOL as apprenticeship sponsor
- Food safety certification

---

## Apprenticeship Sponsor Requirements

Participating restaurants/hotels must:

- Hold valid food service license
- Employ certified chef/kitchen manager
- Maintain health department standards
- Provide safe working environment
- Register with U.S. Department of Labor
- Commit to mentoring apprentice

---

## Compensation

Apprentices earn wages while training:

- **Phase 1 (Months 1-4):** 50-60% of line cook rate
- **Phase 2 (Months 5-10):** 60-75% of line cook rate
- **Phase 3 (Months 11-18):** 75-90% of line cook rate

**Additional Benefits:** Meals during shifts, tips (where applicable)

---

## Attendance Policy

- Required attendance: 95% for classroom, 100% for on-the-job training
- Kitchen operations require reliability
- Tardiness affects service and team
- Professional behavior required at all times

## Program Completion Requirements

- Complete 1,500-2,000 hours (300 classroom + 1,200-1,700 on-the-job)
- Pass all classroom exams (70%)
- Earn ServSafe Manager certification
- Demonstrate competency in all cooking techniques
- Receive satisfactory supervisor evaluations
- Complete portfolio of dishes
- Maintain professional conduct
