# Medical Assistant

## Program Syllabus

**Provider:** Elevate for Humanity  
**ETPL Provider ID:** 10000949  
**Program Code:** MA-CERT  
**Duration:** 16-24 Weeks (320-480 Hours)  
**Delivery Method:** Hybrid (Online Theory + Clinical Practicum)  
**Funding:** WRG • WIOA • Workforce Grants

---

## Program Overview

Train for a healthcare career through hands-on clinical practice. Learn vital signs, EKG, phlebotomy, medical records, and patient care. Hybrid program combines online coursework with real clinic experience, preparing students for immediate employment in doctors' offices, hospitals, and urgent care centers.

---

## Learning Objectives

Upon successful completion, students will be able to:

1. Perform clinical procedures including vital signs, EKG, and phlebotomy
2. Assist physicians with patient examinations and minor procedures
3. Maintain accurate electronic health records (EHR)
4. Apply infection control and safety protocols
5. Demonstrate professional communication with patients and healthcare teams
6. Schedule appointments and manage medical office operations
7. Process insurance claims and medical billing basics
8. Administer medications under physician supervision (where state law permits)

---

## Course Outline

### Module 1: Medical Terminology & Anatomy (40 hours)

- Body systems and medical terminology
- Anatomical positions and directional terms
- Disease processes and pathophysiology
- Medical abbreviations and symbols
- Pharmacology basics

### Module 2: Clinical Skills I - Vital Signs & Patient Care (60 hours)

- Temperature, pulse, respiration, blood pressure
- Height, weight, and BMI calculations
- Patient positioning and draping
- Aseptic technique and infection control
- Hand hygiene and PPE
- Patient safety and fall prevention

### Module 3: Clinical Skills II - EKG & Phlebotomy (80 hours)

- Cardiovascular system review
- 12-lead EKG placement and interpretation
- Holter monitor application
- Venipuncture techniques
- Capillary puncture (finger stick)
- Specimen collection and handling
- Laboratory safety

### Module 4: Medical Office Procedures (40 hours)

- Appointment scheduling systems
- Medical records management
- HIPAA compliance and patient privacy
- Telephone etiquette and triage
- Insurance verification and authorization
- Medical billing and coding basics (ICD-10, CPT)
- Office equipment operation

### Module 5: Electronic Health Records (EHR) (30 hours)

- EHR navigation and documentation
- Patient registration and demographics
- Clinical documentation standards
- E-prescribing basics
- Lab and imaging results management
- Meaningful Use requirements

### Module 6: Pharmacology & Medication Administration (40 hours)

- Drug classifications and actions
- Dosage calculations
- Medication administration routes
- Prescription processing
- Medication safety and error prevention
- Controlled substances regulations

### Module 7: Clinical Practicum (120-240 hours)

- Supervised clinical experience in healthcare facility
- Patient interaction and care
- Clinical procedures under supervision
- Medical office operations
- Professional behavior and ethics
- Preceptor evaluation and feedback

---

## Assessment Methods

- **Module Exams:** 70% passing required for each module
- **Skills Competency Checklists:** Demonstrated proficiency in all clinical skills
- **EHR Simulation:** Practical application assessment
- **Clinical Practicum Evaluation:** Preceptor assessment of performance
- **Final Comprehensive Exam:** 75% passing required
- **National Certification Exam Preparation:** CCMA or RMA exam readiness

---

## Grading Scale

- **A:** 90-100% - Excellent clinical competency
- **B:** 80-89% - Good clinical skills
- **C:** 70-79% - Satisfactory performance
- **F:** Below 70% - Does not meet standards

**Minimum passing grade:** 70% theory, demonstrated competency in all clinical skills

## Instructor Qualifications

All instructors hold:

- Current medical assistant certification (CMA, RMA, or CCMA)
- Minimum 5 years clinical experience
- Teaching credential or adult education experience
- Current CPR/BLS certification
- Active state licensure (if applicable)

## Clinical Site Requirements

Students complete practicum at approved healthcare facilities:

- Physician offices (family practice, internal medicine, pediatrics)
- Urgent care centers
- Hospital outpatient clinics
- Community health centers

**Clinical Hours:** Minimum 120 hours (part-time program) or 240 hours (full-time program)

## Attendance Policy

## Certifications Earned

Students are prepared to sit for:

1. **Certified Clinical Medical Assistant (CCMA)** - NHA
2. **Registered Medical Assistant (RMA)** - AMT
3. **Certified Medical Assistant (CMA)** - AAMA (with additional requirements)
4. **CPR/BLS Certification** - American Heart Association
